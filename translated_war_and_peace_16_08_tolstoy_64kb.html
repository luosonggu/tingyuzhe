﻿CHAPTER VIII

第八章

One matter connected with his management sometimes worried Nicholas , and that was his quick temper together with his old hussar habit of making free use of his fists . At first he saw nothing reprehensible in this , but in the second year of his marriage his view of that form of punishment suddenly changed .

与他的管理有关的一件事有时会让尼古拉斯忧心忡忡，那就是他的急躁脾气和随意使用拳头的轻骑兵老习惯。起初，他认为这没什么可指责的，但到了结婚第二年，他对这种惩罚方式的看法突然改变了。

Once in summer he had sent for the village elder from Bogucharovo , a man who had succeeded to the post when Dron died and who was accused of dishonesty and various irregularities . Nicholas went out into the porch to question him , and immediately after the elder had given a few replies the sound of cries and blows were heard . On returning to lunch Nicholas went up to his wife , who sat with her head bent low over her embroidery frame , and as usual began to tell her what he had been doing that morning . Among other things he spoke of the Bogucharovo elder . Countess Mary turned red and then pale , but continued to sit with head bowed and lips compressed and gave her husband no reply .

有一次夏天，他从博古恰罗沃（Bogucharovo）请来了村里的长老，此人是德隆去世后继任的，被指控不诚实和有各种违规行为。尼古拉斯走到门廊里质问他，长老刚回答了几句，就听到了哭声和殴打声。吃完午饭，尼古拉斯走到妻子跟前，妻子低着头坐在绣架上，尼古拉斯像往常一样开始向她讲述自己今天上午所做的事情。除其他事情外，他还谈到了博古恰罗沃长老。玛丽伯爵夫人脸色一下子变得通红，一下子变得苍白，但还是低着头，紧闭着嘴唇，没有回答丈夫的话。

" Such an insolent scoundrel !" he cried , growing hot again at the mere recollection of him . " If he had told me he was drunk and did not see ... But what is the matter with you , Mary ?" he suddenly asked .

"这样一个无礼的恶棍！"他喊道，只要一想起他，他就又变得火冒三丈。"如果他告诉我他喝醉了，没看见......你怎么了，玛丽？"他突然问道。

Countess Mary raised her head and tried to speak , but hastily looked down again and her lips puckered .

玛丽伯爵夫人抬起头，想要说话，但又急忙低下头，嘴唇撅了起来。

" Why , whatever is the matter , my dearest ?"

"怎么了，有什么事吗，我最亲爱的？"

The looks of the plain Countess Mary always improved when she was in tears . She never cried from pain or vexation , but always from sorrow or pity , and when she wept her radiant eyes acquired an irresistible charm .

朴实的玛丽伯爵夫人在流泪的时候，神情总是很好。她从来不会因为痛苦或烦恼而哭泣，而总是因为悲伤或怜悯而哭泣，当她流泪时，她那神采奕奕的眼睛就会散发出一种难以抗拒的魅力。

The moment Nicholas took her hand she could no longer restrain herself and began to cry .

尼古拉斯握住她手的那一刻，她再也无法克制自己，开始哭泣。

" Nicholas , I saw it ... he was to blame , but why do you ... Nicholas !" and she covered her face with her hands .

"尼古拉斯，我看到了......他是咎由自取，可你为什么尼古拉斯！"她用手捂住了脸。

Nicholas said nothing . He flushed crimson , left her side , and paced up and down the room . He understood what she was weeping about , but could not in his heart at once agree with her that what he had regarded from childhood as quite an everyday event was wrong . " Is it just sentimentality , old wives ' tales , or is she right ?" he asked himself . Before he had solved that point he glanced again at her face filled with love and pain , and he suddenly realized that she was right and that he had long been sinning against himself .

尼古拉斯一言不发。他脸色绯红，离开她身边，在房间里来回踱步。他明白她在哭泣什么，但心里却无法立即同意她的说法，即他从小就认为是很平常的事情是错误的。"他问自己："这究竟是多愁善感、老太太的故事，还是她说得对？在他还没有解决这个问题之前，他又瞥了一眼她那张充满爱和痛苦的脸，他突然意识到她是对的，而他长期以来一直在对自己犯罪。

" Mary ," he said softly , going up to her , " it will never happen again ; I give you my word . Never ," he repeated in a trembling voice like a boy asking for forgiveness .

"玛丽，"他走到她身边，轻声说道，"我向你保证，这种事再也不会发生了。永远不会了，"他用颤抖的声音重复道，就像一个请求原谅的孩子。

The tears flowed faster still from the countess ' eyes . She took his hand and kissed it .

伯爵夫人的眼泪流得更快了。她握住他的手，亲吻着。

" Nicholas , when did you break your cameo ?" she asked to change the subject , looking at his finger on which he wore a ring with a cameo of Laocoon's head .

"尼古拉斯，你是什么时候弄坏你的浮雕的？"为了转移话题，她看着他戴着拉奥孔头像浮雕戒指的手指问道。

" Today — it was the same affair . Oh , Mary , don't remind me of it !" and again he flushed . " I give you my word of honor it shan't occur again , and let this always be a reminder to me ," and he pointed to the broken ring .

"今天--还是那件事。哦，玛丽，别让我想起这件事！"他的脸又一次红了。"我用我的名誉向你保证，这种事不会再发生了，就让这个永远提醒我吧。"他指了指那枚破碎的戒指。

After that , when in discussions with his village elders or stewards the blood rushed to his face and his fists began to clench , Nicholas would turn the broken ring on his finger and would drop his eyes before the man who was making him angry . But he did forget himself once or twice within a twelvemonth , and then he would go and confess to his wife , and would again promise that this should really be the very last time .

从那以后，当尼古拉斯与村里的长老或管家讨论问题时，当他的血液涌上脸庞，拳头开始握紧时，他就会转动手指上的破戒指，然后垂下眼睛，看着那个让他生气的人。但在十二个月内，他确实有一两次忘记了自己，然后他会去向妻子忏悔，并再次保证这真的是最后一次了。

" Mary , you must despise me !" he would say . " I deserve it ."

"玛丽，你一定看不起我！"他会说"我活该"

" You should go , go away at once , if you don't feel strong enough to control yourself ," she would reply sadly , trying to comfort her husband .

"你应该走，马上走，如果你觉得自己控制不了自己的话。"她悲伤地回答，试图安慰丈夫。

Among the gentry of the province Nicholas was respected but not liked . He did not concern himself with the interests of his own class , and consequently some thought him proud and others thought him stupid . The whole summer , from spring sowing to harvest , he was busy with the work on his farm . In autumn he gave himself up to hunting with the same business - like seriousness — leaving home for a month , or even two , with his hunt . In winter he visited his other villages or spent his time reading . The books he read were chiefly historical , and on these he spent a certain sum every year . He was collecting , as he said , a serious library , and he made it a rule to read through all the books he bought . He would sit in his study with a grave air , reading — a task he first imposed upon himself as a duty , but which afterwards became a habit affording him a special kind of pleasure and a consciousness of being occupied with serious matters . In winter , except for business excursions , he spent most of his time at home making himself one with his family and entering into all the details of his children's relations with their mother . The harmony between him and his wife grew closer and closer and he daily discovered fresh spiritual treasures in her .

在该省的贵族中，尼古拉斯受人尊敬，但并不受欢迎。他不关心本阶级的利益，因此有人认为他骄傲，有人认为他愚蠢。整个夏天，从春播到秋收，他都忙于农活。到了秋天，他又以同样认真的态度投入到狩猎活动中，与猎物一起离家一个月，甚至两个月。冬天，他到其他村庄去，或者花时间读书。他读的主要是历史类书籍，每年他都要在这些书上花上一笔钱。正如他所说，他正在收集一个严肃的图书馆，他规定要通读他买的所有书籍。他会神情严肃地坐在书房里读书--他最初把这项任务当作一种责任强加给自己，但后来却成了一种习惯，给他带来了一种特殊的乐趣，让他意识到自己在处理严肃的事情。冬天，除了出差，他大部分时间都待在家里，与家人融为一体，关心孩子们与母亲相处的所有细节。他和妻子之间的关系越来越融洽，他每天都能在妻子身上发现新的精神财富。

From the time of his marriage Sonya had lived in his house . Before that , Nicholas had told his wife all that had passed between himself and Sonya , blaming himself and commending her . He had asked Princess Mary to be gentle and kind to his cousin . She thoroughly realized the wrong he had done Sonya , felt herself to blame toward her , and imagined that her wealth had influenced Nicholas ' choice . She could not find fault with Sonya in any way and tried to be fond of her , but often felt ill - will toward her which she could not overcome .

从结婚起，索尼娅就一直住在他家。在此之前，尼古拉把自己和索尼娅之间发生的一切都告诉了妻子，既责备自己，又赞扬她。他请求玛丽公主对他的表妹温柔体贴。玛丽公主彻底认识到了他对索尼娅做的错事，她觉得自己应该责备索尼娅，并认为她的财富影响了尼古拉斯的选择。她在任何方面都找不到索尼娅的缺点，并努力去喜欢她，但她常常对索尼娅心怀不满，这是她无法克服的。

Once she had a talk with her friend Natasha about Sonya and about her own injustice toward her .

有一次，她和朋友娜塔莎谈起了索尼娅和自己对她的不公。

" You know ," said Natasha , " you have read the Gospels a great deal — there is a passage in them that just fits Sonya ."

"你知道吗，"娜塔莎说，"你读过很多《福音书》，其中有一段话很适合索尼娅。"

" What ?" asked Countess Mary , surprised .

"什么？"玛丽伯爵夫人惊讶地问。

"' To him that hath shall be given , and from him that hath not shall be taken away .' You remember ? She is one that hath not ; why , I don't know . Perhaps she lacks egotism , I don't know , but from her is taken away , and everything has been taken away . Sometimes I am dreadfully sorry for her . Formerly I very much wanted Nicholas to marry her , but I always had a sort of presentiment that it would not come off . She is a sterile flower , you know — like some strawberry blossoms . Sometimes I am sorry for her , and sometimes I think she doesn't feel it as you or I would ."

"'有的要给他，没有的要夺过来。你还记得吗？她就是一个没有的人，为什么，我不知道。也许她缺乏自我意识，我不知道，但她的一切都被剥夺了。有时我真为她感到难过。以前我非常希望尼古拉斯能娶她，但我总预感到这是不可能的。她是一朵不结果实的花，你知道，就像草莓花一样。有时我为她感到遗憾 有时我又觉得她不像你我一样有这种感觉"

Though Countess Mary told Natasha that those words in the Gospel must be understood differently , yet looking at Sonya she agreed with Natasha's explanation . It really seemed that Sonya did not feel her position trying , and had grown quite reconciled to her lot as a sterile flower . She seemed to be fond not so much of individuals as of the family as a whole . Like a cat , she had attached herself not to the people but to the home . She waited on the old countess , petted and spoiled the children , was always ready to render the small services for which she had a gift , and all this was unconsciously accepted from her with insufficient gratitude .

虽然玛丽伯爵夫人告诉娜塔莎，对《福音书》中的这些话必须有不同的理解，但看着索尼娅，她还是同意了娜塔莎的解释。看来，索尼娅并不觉得自己的处境有多艰难，她已经逐渐适应了自己作为一朵不育之花的命运。她喜欢的似乎不是个人，而是整个家庭。她就像一只猫，依恋的不是人，而是家。她伺候老伯爵夫人，宠爱和溺爱孩子们，随时准备提供她有天赋的小服务，而这一切都被她无意识地接受了，却没有足够的感激之情。

The country seat at Bald Hills had been rebuilt , though not on the same scale as under the old prince .

位于秃头山的乡间别墅已经重建，但重建的规模与老亲王时期的不尽相同。

The buildings , begun under straitened circumstances , were more than simple . The immense house on the old stone foundations was of wood , plastered only inside . It had bare deal floors and was furnished with very simple hard sofas , armchairs , tables , and chairs made by their own serf carpenters out of their own birchwood . The house was spacious and had rooms for the house serfs and apartments for visitors . Whole families of the Rostovs ' and Bolkonskis ' relations sometimes came to Bald Hills with sixteen horses and dozens of servants and stayed for months . Besides that , four times a year , on the name days and birthdays of the hosts , as many as a hundred visitors would gather there for a day or two . The rest of the year life pursued its unbroken routine with its ordinary occupations , and its breakfasts , lunches , dinners , and suppers , provided out of the produce of the estate .

这些建筑是在拮据的情况下开始修建的，但并不简单。在旧石基上的巨大房屋是木制的，只在内部抹了灰。屋内铺着光秃秃的木地板，摆放着非常简单的硬沙发、扶手椅、桌子和椅子，都是农奴木匠用自家的桦木制作的。房子很宽敞，既有农奴的房间，也有访客的居室。罗斯托夫家族和博尔孔斯基家族的亲戚们有时会带着 16 匹马和几十个仆人来到秃头山，一住就是几个月。除此之外，每年还有四次，在主人的命名日和生日时，多达上百名游客会聚集在这里一两天。一年中的其余时间，庄园里的人们照常工作，用庄园里的农产品提供早餐、午餐、晚餐和夜宵。
