﻿CHAPTER XII

第十二章

Shortly after nine o'clock that evening , Weyrother drove with his plans to Kutuzov's quarters where the council of war was to be held . All the commanders of columns were summoned to the commander - in - chief's and with the exception of Prince Bagration , who declined to come , were all there at the appointed time .

当晚九点刚过，韦罗瑟就带着他的计划驱车前往库图佐夫的驻地，那里将召开作战会议。所有纵队司令都被召集到了总司令部，除了巴格拉季昂亲王拒绝前来外，其他人都在指定时间到齐了。

Weyrother , who was in full control of the proposed battle , by his eagerness and briskness presented a marked contrast to the dissatisfied and drowsy Kutuzov , who reluctantly played the part of chairman and president of the council of war . Weyrother evidently felt himself to be at the head of a movement that had already become unrestrainable . He was like a horse running downhill harnessed to a heavy cart . Whether he was pulling it or being pushed by it he did not know , but rushed along at headlong speed with no time to consider what this movement might lead to . Weyrother had been twice that evening to the enemy's picket line to reconnoiter personally , and twice to the Emperors , Russian and Austrian , to report and explain , and to his headquarters where he had dictated the dispositions in German , and now , much exhausted , he arrived at Kutuzov's .

韦罗瑟完全掌控了拟议中的战斗，他的急切和爽快与库图佐夫的不满和昏昏欲睡形成了鲜明对比，后者勉强扮演了战争委员会主席的角色。韦罗瑟显然觉得自己是一场运动的领导者，而这场运动已经变得不可收拾。他就像一匹套在沉重的大车上向山下奔跑的马。他不知道自己是在拉车，还是被车推着，只是一股脑儿地往前冲，根本无暇顾及这场运动可能导致的结果。当天晚上，韦罗瑟曾两次亲自前往敌军纠察线侦察，两次向俄奥两国皇帝汇报和解释，并前往他的司令部，在那里用德语口述了部署，现在他筋疲力尽地来到了库图佐夫的司令部。

He was evidently so busy that he even forgot to be polite to the commander in chief . He interrupted him , talked rapidly and indistinctly , without looking at the man he was addressing , and did not reply to questions put to him . He was bespattered with mud and had a pitiful , weary , and distracted air , though at the same time he was haughty and self -confident.

他显然忙得不可开交，甚至忘记了对总司令的礼貌。他打断了他的话，说话又快又含糊，看都不看他正在讲话的人，也不回答向他提出的问题。他满脸是泥，一副可怜、疲惫和心不在焉的样子，尽管他同时又是傲慢和自信的。

Kutuzov was occupying a nobleman's castle of modest dimensions near Ostralitz . In the large drawing room which had become the commander in chief's office were gathered Kutuzov himself , Weyrother , and the members of the council of war . They were drinking tea , and only awaited Prince Bagration to begin the council . At last Bagration's orderly came with the news that the prince could not attend . Prince Andrew came in to inform the commander - in - chief of this and , availing himself of permission previously given him by Kutuzov to be present at the council , he remained in the room .

库图佐夫住在奥斯特拉里茨附近一座面积不大的贵族城堡里。在已成为总司令办公室的大客厅里，聚集着库图佐夫本人、韦罗瑟和作战委员会的成员。他们正在喝茶，只等着巴格拉季昂亲王宣布会议开始。最后，巴格拉季昂的勤务兵带来了王子无法出席的消息。安德鲁王子进来向总司令通报了这一消息，并利用库图佐夫之前给他的出席会议的许可，留在了房间里。

" Since Prince Bagration is not coming , we may begin ," said Weyrother , hurriedly rising from his seat and going up to the table on which an enormous map of the environs of Brunn was spread out .

韦罗瑟说："既然巴格拉季昂亲王不来，我们可以开始了。"他急忙从座位上站起来，走到桌子前，桌子上铺着一张巨大的布伦周边地图。

Kutuzov , with his uniform unbuttoned so that his fat neck bulged over his collar as if escaping , was sitting almost asleep in a low chair , with his podgy old hands resting symmetrically on its arms . At the sound of Weyrother's voice , he opened his one eye with an effort .

库图佐夫解开了军装的扣子，肥胖的脖子从领口处鼓了出来，好像要逃跑似的，他坐在一把矮椅子上几乎睡着了，一双豆荚大的老手对称地搭在椅子的扶手上。听到韦洛瑟的声音，他费力地睁开了独眼。

" Yes , yes , if you please ! It is already late ," said he , and nodding his head he let it droop and again closed his eye .

"是的，是的，如果您愿意的话！已经很晚了。"说着，他点了点头，让头垂了下去，再次闭上了眼睛。

If at first the members of the council thought that Kutuzov was pretending to sleep , the sounds his nose emitted during the reading that followed proved that the commander - in - chief at that moment was absorbed by a far more serious matter than a desire to show his contempt for the dispositions or anything else — he was engaged in satisfying the irresistible human need for sleep . He really was asleep . Weyrother , with the gesture of a man too busy to lose a moment , glanced at Kutuzov and , having convinced himself that he was asleep , took up a paper and in a loud , monotonous voice began to read out the dispositions for the impending battle , under a heading which he also read out :

如果说议会成员们起初以为库图佐夫是在装睡的话，那么他的鼻子在随后的诵读中发出的声音证明，此时此刻，这位总司令正沉浸在一件远比蔑视军情或其他任何事情更严重的事情中--他正在满足人类不可抗拒的睡眠需求。他真的睡着了。韦罗瑟一副忙得不可开交的样子，瞥了库图佐夫一眼，确信他已经睡着了，便拿起一张纸，用响亮而单调的声音开始宣读即将进行的战斗的部署，他还在标题下宣读了以下内容

" Dispositions for an attack on the enemy position behind Kobelnitz and Sokolnitz , November 30, 1805."

"1805年11月30日进攻科贝尔尼茨和索科尔尼茨后方敌军阵地的部署"。

The dispositions were very complicated and difficult . They began as follows :

这些部署非常复杂和困难。开头如下

" As the enemy's left wing rests on wooded hills and his right extends along Kobelnitz and Sokolnitz behind the ponds that are there , while we , on the other hand , with our left wing by far outflank his right , it is advantageous to attack the enemy's latter wing especially if we occupy the villages of Sokolnitz and Kobelnitz , whereby we can both fall on his flank and pursue him over the plain between Schlappanitz and the Thuerassa forest , avoiding the defiles of Schlappanitz and Bellowitz which cover the enemy's front . For this object it is necessary that ... The first column marches ... The second column marches ... The third column marches ..." and so on , read Weyrother .

"由于敌人的左翼位于树木繁茂的山丘上，右翼沿着科伯尔尼茨和索科尔尼茨延伸到那里的池塘后面，而我们的左翼则远远包抄了敌人的右翼、如果我们占领了索科尔尼茨（Sokolnitz）和科伯尔尼茨（Kobelnitz）的村庄，攻击敌人的后翼就更为有利，这样我们就可以从侧翼攻击敌人，并在施拉帕尼茨（Schlappanitz）和图拉萨森林（Thuerassa forest）之间的平原上追击敌人，避开覆盖敌人正面的施拉帕尼茨（Schlappanitz）和贝洛维茨（Bellowitz）的隘口。为此，必须第一纵队前进第二纵队前进第三纵队前进...... "韦罗瑟如是说。

The generals seemed to listen reluctantly to the difficult dispositions . The tall , fair - haired General Buxhowden stood , leaning his back against the wall , his eyes fixed on a burning candle , and seemed not to listen or even to wish to be thought to listen . Exactly opposite Weyrother , with his glistening wide - open eyes fixed upon him and his mustache twisted upwards , sat the ruddy Miloradovich in a military pose , his elbows turned outwards , his hands on his knees , and his shoulders raised . He remained stubbornly silent , gazing at Weyrother's face , and only turned away his eyes when the Austrian chief of staff finished reading . Then Miloradovich looked round significantly at the other generals . But one could not tell from that significant look whether he agreed or disagreed and was satisfied or not with the arrangements . Next to Weyrother sat Count Langeron who , with a subtle smile that never left his typically southern French face during the whole time of the reading , gazed at his delicate fingers which rapidly twirled by its corners a gold snuffbox on which was a portrait . In the middle of one of the longest sentences , he stopped the rotary motion of the snuffbox , raised his head , and with inimical politeness lurking in the corners of his thin lips interrupted Weyrother , wishing to say something . But the Austrian general , continuing to read , frowned angrily and jerked his elbows , as if to say : " You can tell me your views later , but now be so good as to look at the map and listen ." Langeron lifted his eyes with an expression of perplexity , turned round to Miloradovich as if seeking an explanation , but meeting the latter's impressive but meaningless gaze drooped his eyes sadly and again took to twirling his snuffbox .

将军们似乎很不情愿地听着这些艰难的部署。身材高大、头发白皙的布克豪登将军站着，背靠着墙，眼睛盯着燃烧的蜡烛，似乎没有听，甚至不希望别人认为他在听。韦罗瑟的正对面，面色红润的米罗拉多维奇睁着一双闪闪发光的大眼睛，胡子向上翘着，摆出一副军人的姿势，手肘向外，双手放在膝盖上，肩膀抬起。他始终一言不发，凝视着韦罗瑟的脸，直到奥地利参谋长念完，他才转过眼睛。然后，米洛拉多维奇环顾四周，意味深长地看了看其他将军。但是，从那意味深长的眼神中，人们无法判断他是同意还是不同意，对安排是满意还是不满意。韦罗瑟旁边坐着朗格朗伯爵，在整个宣读过程中，他那典型的法国南方人脸上始终带着微妙的微笑，凝视着自己纤细的手指快速捻动着一个金质鼻烟盒的四角，上面是一幅肖像画。在读到一个最长的句子时，他停止了旋转鼻烟盒的动作，抬起头来，薄薄的嘴唇边潜藏着敌意的礼貌，打断了韦罗瑟的话，想说点什么。但这位奥地利将军还在继续看书，他生气地皱了皱眉头，胳膊肘抽了抽，似乎在说："你可以稍后再告诉我你的观点："你可以稍后再告诉我你的观点，但现在请乖乖地看着地图听我说。"朗格朗抬起眼睛，露出困惑的表情，转过身看向米罗拉多维奇，似乎在寻求解释，但面对后者那令人印象深刻却毫无意义的目光，他悲伤地垂下了眼帘，又开始捻起他的鼻烟盒。

" A geography lesson !" he muttered as if to himself , but loud enough to be heard .

"一堂地理课！"他喃喃自语，但声音大得足以让人听见。

Przebyszewski, with respectful but dignified politeness , held his hand to his ear toward Weyrother , with the air of a man absorbed in attention . Dohkturov , a little man , sat opposite Weyrother , with an assiduous and modest mien , and stooping over the outspread map conscientiously studied the dispositions and the unfamiliar locality . He asked Weyrother several times to repeat words he had not clearly heard and the difficult names of villages . Weyrother complied and Dohkturov noted them down .

普尔热比谢夫斯基恭敬而又庄重地把手放在耳边，向着韦罗瑟，一副全神贯注的样子。多赫图罗夫是个小个子，他坐在韦罗瑟对面，神情专注而谦逊，弯腰俯视着摊开的地图，认真地研究着地图的布局和陌生的地点。他几次要求韦罗瑟重复他没有听清楚的单词和难记的村名。韦罗特尔照做了，多赫图罗夫也记了下来。

When the reading which lasted more than an hour was over , Langeron again brought his snuffbox to rest and , without looking at Weyrother or at anyone in particular , began to say how difficult it was to carry out such a plan in which the enemy's position was assumed to be known , whereas it was perhaps not known , since the enemy was in movement . Langeron's objections were valid but it was obvious that their chief aim was to show General Weyrother — who had read his dispositions with as much self -confidence as if he were addressing school children — that he had to do , not with fools , but with men who could teach him something in military matters .

一个多小时的朗读结束后，朗格朗又把他的鼻烟盒拿了出来，他没有看韦罗瑟，也没有看任何人，就开始说执行这样一个计划是多么困难，因为在这个计划中，敌人的位置是假定已知的，而实际上可能并不知道，因为敌人正在移动。朗格朗的反对意见是有道理的，但很明显，他们的主要目的是向韦罗瑟将军表明，他必须面对的不是傻瓜，而是能在军事方面教他一些东西的人。

When the monotonous sound of Weyrother's voice ceased , Kutuzov opened his eye as a miller wakes up when the soporific drone of the mill wheel is interrupted . He listened to what Langeron said , as if remarking , " So you are still at that silly business !" quickly closed his eye again , and let his head sink still lower .

当韦罗瑟单调的声音停止后，库图佐夫睁开了眼睛，就像磨坊主在磨盘的嗡嗡声被打断后醒来一样。他听着朗格戎的话，好像在说："原来你还在干那傻事！"很快又闭上了眼睛，头低得更低了。

Langeron, trying as virulently as possible to sting Weyrother's vanity as author of the military plan , argued that Bonaparte might easily attack instead of being attacked , and so render the whole of this plan perfectly worthless . Weyrother met all objections with a firm and contemptuous smile , evidently prepared beforehand to meet all objections be they what they might .

朗格朗竭力刺痛韦罗瑟作为军事计划制定者的虚荣心，他认为波拿巴可能会轻易发动进攻，而不是被进攻，这样一来，整个计划就一文不值了。韦罗瑟对所有的反对意见都报以坚定而轻蔑的微笑，显然他事先已经做好了应对所有反对意见的准备。

" If he could attack us , he would have done so today ," said he .

"他说："如果他能攻击我们，他今天就会这么做

" So you think he is powerless ?" said Langeron.

"所以你认为他是无能为力的？"朗格朗说。

" He has forty thousand men at most ," replied Weyrother, with the smile of a doctor to whom an old wife wishes to explain the treatment of a case .

"他最多有四万人，"韦罗瑟微笑着回答，就像一位老夫人想向他解释治疗方法的医生一样。

" In that case he is inviting his doom by awaiting our attack ," said Langeron , with a subtly ironical smile , again glancing round for support to Miloradovich who was near him .

"既然如此，他在等待我们的进攻，这是在自寻死路。"朗格戎带着微妙的讽刺笑容说道，他再次向身边的米罗拉多维奇瞥了一眼，以寻求支持。

But Miloradovich was at that moment evidently thinking of anything rather than of what the generals were disputing about .

但米罗拉多维奇此时显然在想任何事情，而不是将军们正在争论的事情。

" Ma foi !" said he , " tomorrow we shall see all that on the battlefield ."

"妈呀！"他说，"明天我们就能在战场上看到这一切了。"

Weyrother again gave that smile which seemed to say that to him it was strange and ridiculous to meet objections from Russian generals and to have to prove to them what he had not merely convinced himself of , but had also convinced the sovereign Emperors of .

韦罗瑟又露出了那种微笑，似乎在说，对他来说，遇到俄国将军们的反对，还得向他们证明他不仅使自己相信，而且使君主皇帝们相信的事情，真是奇怪而可笑。

" The enemy has quenched his fires and a continual noise is heard from his camp ," said he . " What does that mean ? Either he is retreating , which is the only thing we need fear , or he is changing his position ." (He smiled ironically.) "But even if he also took up a position in the Thuerassa , he merely saves us a great deal of trouble and all our arrangements to the minutest detail remain the same ."

"他说："敌人已经熄灭了战火，营地里不断传来喧闹声。"这意味着什么？要么他在撤退，这是我们唯一需要担心的事情，要么他在改变位置。"(他讽刺地笑了笑。）"不过，即使他也在瑟拉萨占领了一个阵地，他也只是给我们省去了很多麻烦，我们所有的安排到最微小的细节都没有变。"

" How is that ?..." began Prince Andrew , who had for long been waiting an opportunity to express his doubts .

"怎么会这样...... "安德鲁王子开始发问，他早就想找个机会表达自己的疑惑。

Kutuzov here woke up , coughed heavily , and looked round at the generals .

库图佐夫这才醒悟过来，重重地咳嗽了一声，环视了一下诸位将军。

" Gentlemen , the dispositions for tomorrow — or rather for today , for it is past midnight — cannot now be altered ," said he . " You have heard them , and we shall all do our duty . But before a battle , there is nothing more important ..." he paused , " than to have a good sleep ."

"他说："先生们，明天的部署，或者说今天的部署，因为已经过了午夜，现在不能更改了。他说："你们都听到了，我们都会尽自己的职责。但在战斗之前，没有什么比好好睡上一觉更重要的了...... "他停顿了一下，""。

He moved as if to rise . The generals bowed and retired . It was past midnight . Prince Andrew went out .

他站起身来。将军们鞠躬退下。午夜已过。安德鲁王子走了出去。

The council of war , at which Prince Andrew had not been able to express his opinion as he had hoped to , left on him a vague and uneasy impression . Whether Dolgorukov and Weyrother , or Kutuzov , Langeron , and the others who did not approve of the plan of attack , were right — he did not know . " But was it really not possible for Kutuzov to state his views plainly to the Emperor ? Is it possible that on account of court and personal considerations tens of thousands of lives , and my life , my life ," he thought , " must be risked ?"

安德鲁王子未能如愿表达自己的意见，这次战争会议给他留下了模糊而不安的印象。多尔戈鲁科夫和韦罗瑟，或者库图佐夫、朗格朗和其他不赞成进攻计划的人是否正确，他不知道。"但是，难道库图佐夫真的不能向皇帝直截了当地表明自己的观点吗？难道出于宫廷和个人的考虑，数以万计的生命，还有我的生命，我的生命，"他想，"必须冒这个险吗？"

" Yes , it is very likely that I shall be killed tomorrow ," he thought . And suddenly , at this thought of death , a whole series of most distant , most intimate , memories rose in his imagination : he remembered his last parting from his father and his wife ; he remembered the days when he first loved her . He thought of her pregnancy and felt sorry for her and for himself , and in a nervously emotional and softened mood he went out of the hut in which he was billeted with Nesvitski and began to walk up and down before it .

"他想，"是的，明天我很可能会被杀死。突然，一想到死亡，他的想象中就浮现出一系列最遥远、最亲切的回忆：他想起了与父亲和妻子最后的离别；他想起了初恋时的日子。他想到她怀孕了，为她也为自己感到难过。他怀着紧张激动而又柔软的心情，走出他和内斯维茨基住的小屋，开始在小屋前走来走去。

The night was foggy and through the fog the moonlight gleamed mysteriously . " Yes , tomorrow , tomorrow !" he thought . " Tomorrow everything may be over for me ! All these memories will be no more , none of them will have any meaning for me . Tomorrow perhaps , even certainly , I have a presentiment that for the first time I shall have to show all I can do ." And his fancy pictured the battle , its loss , the concentration of fighting at one point , and the hesitation of all the commanders . And then that happy moment , that Toulon for which he had so long waited, presents itself to him at last . He firmly and clearly expresses his opinion to Kutuzov , to Weyrother , and to the Emperors . All are struck by the justness of his views , but no one undertakes to carry them out , so he takes a regiment , a division - stipulates that no one is to interfere with his arrangements — leads his division to the decisive point , and gains the victory alone . " But death and suffering ?" suggested another voice . Prince Andrew , however , did not answer that voice and went on dreaming of his triumphs . The dispositions for the next battle are planned by him alone . Nominally he is only an adjutant on Kutuzov's staff , but he does everything alone . The next battle is won by him alone . Kutuzov is removed and he is appointed ... " Well and then ?" asked the other voice . " If before that you are not ten times wounded , killed , or betrayed , well ... what then ?..." " Well then ," Prince Andrew answered himself , " I don't know what will happen and don't want to know , and can't , but if I want this — want glory , want to be known to men , want to be loved by them , it is not my fault that I want it and want nothing but that and live only for that . Yes , for that alone ! I shall never tell anyone , but , oh God ! what am I to do if I love nothing but fame and men's esteem ? Death , wounds , the loss of family — I fear nothing . And precious and dear as many persons are to me — father , sister , wife — those dearest to me — yet dreadful and unnatural as it seems , I would give them all at once for a moment of glory , of triumph over men, of love from men I don't know and never shall know, for the love of these men here ," he thought , as he listened to voices in Kutuzov's courtyard . The voices were those of the orderlies who were packing up ; one voice , probably a coachman's , was teasing Kutuzov's old cook whom Prince Andrew knew , and who was called Tit . He was saying , " Tit , I say , Tit !"

夜雾弥漫，透过浓雾，月光闪烁着神秘的光芒。"是的，明天，明天！"他想。"明天，我的一切都将结束！所有这些记忆都将不复存在，它们对我都没有任何意义。也许明天，甚至肯定明天，我有一种预感，我将第一次展示我的全部能力"。他幻想着那场战斗、那场战斗的失利、那集中在一点的战斗以及所有指挥官的犹豫不决。然后，那个幸福的时刻，那个他等待已久的土伦，终于出现在他面前。他坚定而明确地向库图佐夫、韦罗瑟和皇帝们表达了自己的意见。所有人都被他的观点所打动，但没有人承诺执行他的观点，于是他带着一个团、一个师--规定任何人不得干涉他的安排--率领他的师到达决定性的地点，独自取得了胜利。"但死亡和痛苦呢？"另一个声音问道。然而，安德鲁王子没有回答那个声音，而是继续做着他的胜利之梦。下一场战斗的部署由他独自策划。名义上，他只是库图佐夫参谋部的一名副官，但所有事情都是他一个人干的。他一个人赢得了下一场战斗。库图佐夫被撤职，他被任命为......"然后呢？"另一个声音问道。"如果在此之前，你没有十次受伤、被杀或背叛，那么......然后呢？""那好吧，"安德鲁王子自言自语道，"我不知道会发生什么，也不想知道，也不能知道，但如果我想要这些--想要荣耀，想要为人所知，想要得到他们的爱，那就不是我的错，我只想要这些，只为这些而活。是的，就是为了这个！我永远不会告诉任何人，但是，上帝啊！如果我只爱名声和人们的尊敬，我该怎么办？死亡、伤痛、失去家人，我什么都不怕。父亲、姐姐、妻子，这些对我来说最宝贵、最亲爱的人，虽然看起来可怕而不合常理，但我愿意为了片刻的荣耀，为了战胜人类，为了我不认识也永远不会认识的人对我的爱，为了这些人对我的爱，立刻把他们都献给他们，"他一边想着，一边倾听着库图佐夫院子里的声音。这些声音是正在收拾行李的勤务兵的声音；有一个声音，可能是马车夫的声音，正在取笑安德鲁王子认识的库图佐夫的老厨子，他叫提特。他说："提特，我说，提特！"

" Well ?" returned the old man .

"怎么样？"老人回道

" Go , Tit , thresh a bit !" said the wag .

"去吧，提特，脱点皮吧！"老巫婆说。

" Oh , go to the devil !" called out a voice , drowned by the laughter of the orderlies and servants .

"哦，去死吧！"一个声音叫道，被勤杂工和仆人们的笑声淹没了。

" All the same , I love and value nothing but triumph over them all , I value this mystic power and glory that is floating here above me in this mist !"

"同样，除了战胜他们，我什么也不爱，什么也不看重，我看重的是这神秘的力量和光荣，它就漂浮在我头顶的迷雾中！"
