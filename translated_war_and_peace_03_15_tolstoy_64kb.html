﻿CHAPTER XV

第十五章

At eight o'clock Kutuzov rode to Pratzen at the head of the fourth column , Miloradovich's , the one that was to take the place of Przebyszewski's and Langeron's columns which had already gone down into the valley . He greeted the men of the foremost regiment and gave them the order to march , thereby indicating that he intended to lead that column himself . When he had reached the village of Pratzen he halted . Prince Andrew was behind , among the immense number forming the commander - in - chief's suite . He was in a state of suppressed excitement and irritation , though controlledly calm as a man is at the approach of a long-awaited moment . He was firmly convinced that this was the day of his Toulon , or his bridge of Arcola . How it would come about he did not know , but he felt sure it would do so . The locality and the position of our troops were known to him as far as they could be known to anyone in our army . His own strategic plan , which obviously could not now be carried out , was forgotten . Now , entering into Weyrother's plan, Prince Andrew considered possible contingencies and formed new projects such as might call for his rapidity of perception and decision .

八点钟，库图佐夫率领第四纵队米洛拉多维奇纵队骑马来到普拉特岑，这支纵队将接替已经进入山谷的普热比谢夫斯基纵队和朗格朗纵队。他向最前面那个团的士兵们问好，并向他们下达了行军命令，以此表明他打算亲自率领这支纵队。到达普拉岑村后，他停了下来。安德鲁王子跟在后面，在总司令的众多随从中。他处于一种压抑不住的激动和烦躁之中，尽管在期待已久的时刻即将到来之际，他仍能保持冷静。他坚信，这就是他的土伦之日，也是他的阿科拉桥之日。他不知道这一天会如何到来，但他确信这一天一定会到来。他对我军的位置和位置了如指掌。他自己的战略计划现在显然无法实施，但他已经忘记了。现在，安德鲁王子进入了韦罗瑟的计划，考虑可能出现的意外情况，并制定了新的计划，这些计划可能需要他快速的洞察力和决断力。

To the left down below in the mist , the musketry fire of unseen forces could be heard . It was there Prince Andrew thought the fight would concentrate . "There we shall encounter difficulties, and there," thought he , " I shall be sent with a brigade or division , and there , standard in hand , I shall go forward and break whatever is in front of me ."

在左下方的迷雾中，可以听到看不见的部队的火枪射击声。安德鲁王子认为，战斗将集中在那里。"他想，"在那里，我们会遇到困难，在那里，我将被派出一个旅或一个师，在那里，我将手持标枪，勇往直前，击溃我面前的一切"。

He could not look calmly at the standards of the passing battalions . Seeing them he kept thinking , " That may be the very standard with which I shall lead the army ."

他无法平静地看着过往各营的标枪。他一直在想："那可能就是我领导军队的标准"。

In the morning all that was left of the night mist on the heights was a hoar frost now turning to dew , but in the valleys it still lay like a milk - white sea . Nothing was visible in the valley to the left into which our troops had descended and from whence came the sounds of firing . Above the heights was the dark clear sky , and to the right the vast orb of the sun . In front , far off on the farther shore of that sea of mist , some wooded hills were discernible , and it was there the enemy probably was , for something could be descried. On the right the Guards were entering the misty region with a sound of hoofs and wheels and now and then a gleam of bayonets ; to the left beyond the village similar masses of cavalry came up and disappeared in the sea of mist . In front and behind moved infantry . The commander - in - chief was standing at the end of the village letting the troops pass by him . That morning Kutuzov seemed worn and irritable . The infantry passing before him came to a halt without any command being given , apparently obstructed by something in front .

清晨，高原上的夜雾只剩下一层正在变成露水的冰霜，但在山谷中，夜雾仍像乳白色的海洋。左边的山谷里什么也看不见，我们的部队就是从那里下山的，枪声也是从那里传来的。高地之上是漆黑的晴空，右边是浩瀚的太阳。前方，在雾海的远处，可以看到一些树木繁茂的山丘，敌人可能就在那里，因为可以看到一些东西。右边是近卫军，他们带着马蹄声和车轮声进入雾区，时不时还能看到刺刀的闪光；左边的村庄外也有类似的骑兵队伍，他们冲上来又消失在雾海之中。步兵在前后移动。总司令站在村尾，任由部队从他身边走过。那天早晨，库图佐夫显得疲惫不堪、烦躁不安。从他面前经过的步兵没有发出任何命令就停了下来，显然是被前面的东西挡住了去路。

" Do order them to form into battalion columns and go round the village !" he said angrily to a general who had ridden up . " Don't you understand , your excellency , my dear sir , that you must not defile through narrow village streets when we are marching against the enemy ?"

"命令他们排成营纵队，绕村前进！"他愤怒地对一名骑马赶来的将军说。"阁下，我亲爱的先生，您难道不明白，在我们向敌人进军时，绝不能在狭窄的村道上隘路吗？"

" I intended to re - form them beyond the village , your excellency ," answered the general .

"我打算在村外重新编组，阁下。"将军回答道。

Kutuzov laughed bitterly.

库图佐夫苦笑了一下。

" You'll make a fine thing of it , deploying in sight of the enemy ! Very fine!"

"你会做得很好的，在敌人的视线范围内部署！非常好！"

" The enemy is still far away , your excellency . According to the dispositions ..."

"敌人还在远处，阁下。根据部署......"

" The dispositions !" exclaimed Kutuzov bitterly . " Who told you that ?... Kindly do as you are ordered ."

"部署！"库图佐夫痛苦地叫道。"谁告诉你的？请按命令行事。"

An Austrian officer in a white uniform with green plumes in his hat galloped up to Kutuzov and asked in the Emperor's name had the fourth column advanced into action .

一位身着白色军服、帽上插着绿色翎子的奥地利军官飞奔到库图佐夫面前，以皇帝的名义询问第四纵队是否已经开始行动。

Kutuzov turned round without answering and his eye happened to fall upon Prince Andrew , who was beside him . Seeing him , Kutuzov's malevolent and caustic expression softened , as if admitting that what was being done was not his adjutant's fault , and still not answering the Austrian adjutant , he addressed Bolkonski .

库图佐夫转过身去，没有回答，他的目光恰好落在身旁的安德鲁王子身上。看到他，库图佐夫恶狠狠的表情缓和下来，似乎承认所做的一切并不是他的副官的错，他仍然没有回答奥地利副官，而是对博尔孔斯基说："去吧，我亲爱的朋友。

" Go , my dear fellow , and see whether the third division has passed the village . Tell it to stop and await my orders ."

"去吧，我亲爱的朋友，去看看第三师是否已经通过村庄。让它停下来，等我的命令。"

Hardly had Prince Andrew started than he stopped him .

安德鲁王子还没起步，就被他拦住了。

" And ask whether sharpshooters have been posted ," he added . " What are they doing ? What are they doing ?" he murmured to himself , still not replying to the Austrian .

"他补充道："再问问是否已经派驻了神枪手。"他们在干什么？他们在干什么？"他喃喃自语，仍然没有回答奥地利人。

Prince Andrew galloped off to execute the order .

安德鲁王子飞奔去执行命令。

Overtaking the battalions that continued to advance , he stopped the third division and convinced himself that there really were no sharpshooters in front of our columns . The colonel at the head of the regiment was much surprised at the commander - in - chief's order to throw out skirmishers . He had felt perfectly sure that there were other troops in front of him and that the enemy must be at least six miles away . There was really nothing to be seen in front except a barren descent hidden by dense mist . Having given orders in the commander - in - chief's name to rectify this omission , Prince Andrew galloped back . Kutuzov still in the same place , his stout body resting heavily in the saddle with the lassitude of age , sat yawning wearily with closed eyes . The troops were no longer moving , but stood with the butts of their muskets on the ground .

他超越了继续前进的各营，拦住了第三师，并确信我们的纵队前面确实没有神枪手。团长上校对总司令下达的投掷小兵的命令感到非常惊讶。他非常确信前方还有其他部队，敌人至少在六英里之外。除了被浓雾遮住的荒凉下坡外，前方真的什么也看不到。安德鲁王子以总司令的名义下达了纠正这一疏忽的命令后，便疾驰而回。库图佐夫还在原地，他那壮硕的身躯因年老体衰而沉重地靠在马鞍上，闭着眼睛疲惫地打着哈欠。部队不再移动，而是端着枪托站在地上。

" All right , all right !" he said to Prince Andrew , and turned to a general who , watch in hand , was saying it was time they started as all the left - flank columns had already descended .

"好了，好了！"他对安德鲁王子说，然后转向一位将军，这位将军手里拿着手表，说他们该出发了，因为所有的左翼纵队都已经下山了。

" Plenty of time , your excellency ," muttered Kutuzov in the midst of a yawn . " Plenty of time ," he repeated .

"时间充裕，阁下。"库图佐夫打着哈欠嘟囔道。"时间充裕，"他重复道。

Just then at a distance behind Kutuzov was heard the sound of regiments saluting , and this sound rapidly came nearer along the whole extended line of the advancing Russian columns . Evidently the person they were greeting was riding quickly . When the soldiers of the regiment in front of which Kutuzov was standing began to shout , he rode a little to one side and looked round with a frown . Along the road from Pratzen galloped what looked like a squadron of horsemen in various uniforms . Two of them rode side by side in front , at full gallop . One in a black uniform with white plumes in his hat rode a bobtailed chestnut horse , the other who was in a white uniform rode a black one . These were the two Emperors followed by their suites . Kutuzov , affecting the manners of an old soldier at the front , gave the command " Attention !" and rode up to the Emperors with a salute. His whole appearance and manner were suddenly transformed . He put on the air of a subordinate who obeys without reasoning . With an affectation of respect which evidently struck Alexander unpleasantly , he rode up and saluted .

就在这时，在库图佐夫身后很远的地方，传来了各团敬礼的声音，这个声音沿着俄军纵队前进的整条延长线迅速靠近。显然，他们要迎接的人骑马很快。当库图佐夫站在前面的那个团的士兵开始喊叫时，他骑着马稍稍偏向一边，皱着眉头环顾四周。在从普拉特岑出发的路上，驰骋着一群身着不同制服的骑兵。其中两人并排骑在前面，全速前进。一位身着黑色制服，帽子上插着白色羽翎，骑着一匹双尾栗色马，另一位身着白色制服，骑着一匹黑色马。这就是两位皇帝及其随从。库图佐夫以前线老兵的风度，发出 "立正！"的口令，骑马向两位皇帝敬礼。他的整个外表和举止突然发生了变化。他摆出一副不假思索地服从命令的下属模样。他装出一副尊敬的样子，显然让亚历山大很不愉快，他骑马上前行礼。

This unpleasant impression merely flitted over the young and happy face of the Emperor like a cloud of haze across a clear sky and vanished . After his illness he looked rather thinner that day than on the field of Olmutz where Bolkonski had seen him for the first time abroad , but there was still the same bewitching combination of majesty and mildness in his fine gray eyes , and on his delicate lips the same capacity for varying expression and the same prevalent appearance of goodhearted innocent youth .

这种不愉快的印象只是在皇帝年轻而快乐的脸上一闪而过，就像晴空中的一缕阴霾，随即消失了。病愈后的亚历山大皇帝那天比博尔孔斯基第一次在国外见到他的奥尔穆茨战场上看起来瘦了许多，但他那双细腻的灰色眼睛里仍旧是威严与温和的迷人结合，他那娇嫩的嘴唇上仍旧是善于变化的表情，仍旧是一副心地善良的天真青年的模样。

At the Olmutz review he had seemed more majestic ; here he seemed brighter and more energetic . He was slightly flushed after galloping two miles , and reining in his horse he sighed restfully and looked round at the faces of his suite , young and animated as his own . Czartoryski, Novosiltsev, Prince Volkonsky, Strogonov, and the others , all richly dressed gay young men on splendid , well -groomed, fresh, only slightly heated horses , exchanging remarks and smiling , had stopped behind the Emperor . The Emperor Francis , a rosy , long faced young man , sat very erect on his handsome black horse , looking about him in a leisurely and preoccupied manner . He beckoned to one of his white adjutants and asked some question —" Most likely he is asking at what o'clock they started ," thought Prince Andrew , watching his old acquaintance with a smile he could not repress as he recalled his reception at Brunn. In the Emperors ' suite were the picked young orderly officers of the Guard and line regiments , Russian and Austrian . Among them were grooms leading the Tsar's beautiful relay horses covered with embroidered cloths .

在奥尔穆茨的检阅中，他显得更加威严；而在这里，他显得更加明朗，更加精力充沛。奔跑了两英里后，他的脸微微泛红，他勒住马缰绳，安详地叹了口气，环视着他的随从，他们和他一样年轻而充满活力。恰尔托雷斯基、诺沃西尔采夫、沃尔孔斯基亲王、斯特罗戈诺夫和其他人，都是衣着华丽的男青年，骑着华丽、整洁、新鲜、只是略微发热的马匹，相互交谈着，面带微笑，停在皇帝身后。弗朗西斯皇帝是个面色红润的长脸年轻人，他非常挺拔地坐在英俊的黑马上，悠闲而专注地环顾四周。他向他的一位白人副官招了招手，问了一些问题--"他很可能是在问他们几点钟出发，"安德鲁王子想，他看着这位老熟人，回想起他在布伦受到的接待，脸上露出了难以抑制的微笑。在皇帝的套间里，有俄罗斯和奥地利的近卫军和近卫团挑选出来的年轻勤务军官。其中有马夫牵着沙皇的漂亮接力马，马身上绣满了绣花。

As when a window is opened a whiff of fresh air from the fields enters a stuffy room , so a whiff of youthfulness , energy , and confidence of success reached Kutuzov's cheerless staff with the galloping advent of all these brilliant young men .

就像打开一扇窗，田野里的新鲜空气就会进入闷热的房间一样，随着这些才华横溢的年轻人的飞奔而来，一股青春、活力和成功信心的气息也传到了库图佐夫无精打采的幕僚席上。

"为什么还不开始，迈克尔-伊拉里奥诺维奇？"亚历山大皇帝急匆匆地对库图佐夫说，同时礼貌地瞥了弗朗西斯皇帝一眼。

"I am waiting, Your Majesty," answered Kutuzov , bending forward respectfully .

"我在等，陛下。"库图佐夫恭敬地弯腰回答。

Miloradovich wheeled his horse sharply and stationed himself a little behind the Emperor . The Apsheron men, excited by the Tsar's presence , passed in step before the Emperors and their suites at a bold, brisk pace.

"乐意效劳！"士兵们喊道。

The Emperor's horse started at the sudden cry . This horse that had carried the sovereign at reviews in Russia bore him also here on the field of Austerlitz , enduring the heedless blows of his left foot and pricking its ears at the sound of shots just as it had done on the Empress ' Field , not understanding the significance of the firing , nor of the nearness of the Emperor Francis ' black cob , nor of all that was being said , thought , and felt that day by its rider .

皇帝的战马被这突如其来的喊声惊起。这匹曾在俄国驮着君主接受检阅的战马，在奥斯特利茨战场上也驮着他，忍受着他左脚不经意的击打，听到枪声就竖起耳朵，就像在女皇的战场上一样，它不明白枪声的意义，也不明白弗朗西斯皇帝的黑马就在附近，更不明白骑手那天所说的、所想的和所感受到的一切。

The Emperor turned with a smile to one of his followers and made a remark to him , pointing to the gallant Apsherons.

皇帝微笑着转向他的一名随从，指着英勇的阿普舍龙对他说了一句话。
