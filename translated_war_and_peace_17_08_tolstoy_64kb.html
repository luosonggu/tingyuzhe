﻿CHAPTER VIII

第八章

If history dealt only with external phenomena , the establishment of this simple and obvious law would suffice and we should have finished our argument . But the law of history relates to man . A particle of matter cannot tell us that it does not feel the law of attraction or repulsion and that that law is untrue , but man , who is the subject of history , says plainly : I am free and am therefore not subject to the law .

如果历史只涉及外部现象，那么确立这一简单而明显的规律就足够了，我们也就完成了我们的论证。但是，历史规律与人有关。一粒物质无法告诉我们它感受不到吸引力或排斥力的规律，也无法告诉我们这一规律是不真实的，但作为历史主体的人却直截了当地说："我是自由的，因此不受任何约束：我是自由的，因此不受法律约束。

The presence of the problem of man's free will , though unexpressed , is felt at every step of history .

人的自由意志问题虽然没有表达出来，但历史的每一步都能感受到它的存在。

All seriously thinking historians have involuntarily encountered this question . All the contradictions and obscurities of history and the false path historical science has followed are due solely to the lack of a solution of that question .

所有认真思考的历史学家都会不由自主地遇到这个问题。历史上所有的矛盾和晦涩，以及历史科学所走过的错误道路，都完全是由于缺乏对这一问题的解决。

If the will of every man were free , that is , if each man could act as he pleased , all history would be a series of disconnected incidents .

如果每个人的意志都是自由的，也就是说，如果每个人都可以随心所欲地行事，那么所有的历史都将是一系列互不关联的事件。

If in a thousand years even one man in a million could act freely , that is , as he chose , it is evident that one single free act of that man's in violation of the laws governing human action would destroy the possibility of the existence of any laws for the whole of humanity .

如果在一千年中，即使一百万人中有一个人可以自由行动，即随心所欲，那么很显然，这个人的一个违反人类行动法则的自由行动就会破坏整个人类存在任何法则的可能性。

If there be a single law governing the actions of men , free will cannot exist , for then man's will is subject to that law .

如果存在支配人类行动的单一法则，自由意志就不可能存在，因为这样一来，人的意志就会受制于该法则。

In this contradiction lies the problem of free will , which from most ancient times has occupied the best human minds and from most ancient times has been presented in its whole tremendous significance .

自古以来，自由意志的问题就一直困扰着人类最优秀的思想，自古以来，自由意志的问题就一直以其巨大的意义呈现在人们面前。

The problem is that regarding man as a subject of observation from whatever point of view — theological , historical , ethical , or philosophic — we find a general law of necessity to which he ( like all that exists) is subject . But regarding him from within ourselves as what we are conscious of , we feel ourselves to be free .

问题在于，无论从神学、历史学、伦理学或哲学的角度，把人作为一个观察对象，我们都会发现他（像所有存在的事物一样）受制于一个普遍的必然规律。但是，从我们的内心将其视为我们所意识到的东西，我们就会感到自己是自由的。

This consciousness is a source of self - cognition quite apart from and independent of reason . Through his reason man observes himself , but only through consciousness does he know himself .

这种意识是自我认知的源泉，与理性截然不同，也独立于理性。人通过理性观察自己，但只有通过意识才能认识自己。

Apart from consciousness of self no observation or application of reason is conceivable .

离开了自我意识，就无法进行观察或运用理性。

To understand , observe , and draw conclusions , man must first of all be conscious of himself as living . A man is only conscious of himself as a living being by the fact that he wills , that is , is conscious of his volition . But his will — which forms the essence of his life — man recognizes (and can but recognize) as free .

要理解、观察和得出结论，人首先必须意识到自己是活着的。一个人只有通过他的意志，即意识到自己的意志，才能意识到自己是一个活生生的人。但是，他的意志--这构成了他生命的本质--是人所认识到的（而且只能认识到）是自由的。

If , observing himself , man sees that his will is always directed by one and the same law (whether he observes the necessity of taking food, using his brain, or anything else) he cannot recognize this never - varying direction of his will otherwise than as a limitation of it . Were it not free it could not be limited . A man's will seems to him to be limited just because he is not conscious of it except as free .

如果人在观察自己的时候，发现自己的意志总是受同一条法则的支配（无论他观察到的是进食的必要性、用脑的必要性，还是其他任何事物的必要性），那么他就不能不承认他的意志的这种永不改变的支配是对它的限制。如果意志不是自由的，它就不会受到限制。一个人的意志在他看来是有限的，就因为他意识不到它是自由的。

You say : I am not free . But I have lifted my hand and let it fall . Everyone understands that this illogical reply is an irrefutable demonstration of freedom .

你说我不自由但我举起了手，又让它落下。每个人都明白，这个不合逻辑的回答无可辩驳地证明了自由。

That reply is the expression of a consciousness that is not subject to reason .

这个回答表达了一种不受理性支配的意识。

If the consciousness of freedom were not a separate and independent source of self - consciousness it would be subject to reasoning and to experience , but in fact such subjection does not exist and is inconceivable .

如果自由的意识不是一个独立的自我意识源泉，它就会受制于推理和经验，但事实上，这种受制并不存在，也是不可想象的。

A series of experiments and arguments proves to every man that he , as an object of observation , is subject to certain laws , and man submits to them and never resists the laws of gravity or impermeability once he has become acquainted with them . But the same series of experiments and arguments proves to him that the complete freedom of which he is conscious in himself is impossible , and that his every action depends on his organization , his character , and the motives acting upon him ; yet man never submits to the deductions of these experiments and arguments . Having learned from experiment and argument that a stone falls downwards , a man indubitably believes this and always expects the law that he has learned to be fulfilled .

一系列的实验和论证向每个人证明，他作为一个观察对象，受制于某些规律，而人一旦熟悉了这些规律，就会服从这些规律，永远不会抗拒万有引力或不可渗透的规律。但是，同样一系列的实验和论证向他证明，他意识到的自身的完全自由是不可能的，他的每一个行为都取决于他的组织、他的性格和作用于他的动机；然而，人却从不服从这些实验和论证的推论。从实验和论证中得知石头会往下掉，人就会坚信这一点，并总是期望他所学到的规律能够实现。

But learning just as certainly that his will is subject to laws , he does not and cannot believe this .

但是，当他同样肯定地知道他的意志受制于规律时，他就不会也不可能相信这

However often experiment and reasoning may show a man that under the same conditions and with the same character he will do the same thing as before , yet when under the same conditions and with the same character he approaches for the thousandth time the action that always ends in the same way , he feels as certainly convinced as before the experiment that he can act as he pleases . Every man , savage or sage , however incontestably reason and experiment may prove to him that it is impossible to imagine two different courses of action in precisely the same conditions , feels that without this irrational conception ( which constitutes the essence of freedom ) he cannot imagine life . He feels that however impossible it may be , it is so , for without this conception of freedom not only would he be unable to understand life , but he would be unable to live for a single moment .

无论实验和推理多么频繁地告诉一个人，在同样的条件下，具有同样性格的他会做和以前一样的事情，但是当他在同样的条件下，具有同样性格的他第一千次接近总是以同样方式结束的行动时，他就会感到和实验前一样确信，他可以随心所欲地行动。每一个人，不管是野蛮人还是圣人，不管理性和实验如何无可争辩地向他证明，在完全相同的条件下想象两种不同的行动方式是不可能的，他都会感到，如果没有这种非理性的观念（它构成了自由的本质），他就无法想象生活。他觉得，无论多么不可能，事实就是如此，因为如果没有这种自由的概念，他不仅无法理解生活，而且无法生活片刻。

He could not live , because all man's efforts , all his impulses to life , are only efforts to increase freedom . Wealth and poverty , fame and obscurity , power and subordination , strength and weakness , health and disease , culture and ignorance , work and leisure , repletion and hunger , virtue and vice , are only greater or lesser degrees of freedom .

他无法生活，因为人的一切努力，人对生活的一切冲动，都只是为了增加自由。财富与贫穷、名声与蒙昧、权力与从属、力量与虚弱、健康与疾病、文化与无知、工作与闲暇、充实与饥饿、美德与恶习，都只是自由程度的高低而已。

A man having no freedom cannot be conceived of except as deprived of life .

一个没有自由的人，除了被剥夺生命之外，是无法想象的

If the conception of freedom appears to reason to be a senseless contradiction like the possibility of performing two actions at one and the same instant of time , or of an effect without a cause , that only proves that consciousness is not subject to reason .

如果自由的概念在理性看来是一种无意义的矛盾，就像在同一时刻做出两种行为的可能性，或者是一种没有原因的结果，那只能证明意识不受理性的支配。

This unshakable , irrefutable consciousness of freedom , uncontrolled by experiment or argument , recognized by all thinkers and felt by everyone without exception , this consciousness without which no conception of man is possible constitutes the other side of the question .

这种不可动摇、无可辩驳的自由意识，不受实验或论证的控制，为所有思想家所认可，每个人都能毫无例外地感受到，没有这种意识，就不可能有关于人的任何概念，这就是问题的另一面。

Man is the creation of an all -powerful, all - good , and all - seeing God . What is sin , the conception of which arises from the consciousness of man's freedom ? That is a question for theology .

人是全能、全善、全视的上帝的创造物。罪的概念源于人的自由意识，那么什么是罪呢？这是神学的问题。

The actions of men are subject to general immutable laws expressed in statistics . What is man's responsibility to society , the conception of which results from the conception of freedom ? That is a question for jurisprudence .

人的行为受制于以统计数据为表现形式的永恒不变的普遍规律。人对社会的责任是什么？这是法理学的问题。

Man's actions proceed from his innate character and the motives acting upon him . What is conscience and the perception of right and wrong in actions that follows from the consciousness of freedom ? That is a question for ethics .

人的行为源于他与生俱来的性格和作用于他的动机。什么是自由意识所产生的良知和行为是非观？这是伦理学的问题。

Man in connection with the general life of humanity appears subject to laws which determine that life . But the same man apart from that connection appears to be free . How should the past life of nations and of humanity be regarded — as the result of the free , or as the result of the constrained , activity of man ? That is a question for history .

与人类的普遍生活相关联的人似乎受制于决定这种生活的法则。但脱离了这种联系的同一个人似乎是自由的。应该如何看待民族和人类过去的生活--是人类自由活动的结果，还是人类受约束活动的结果？这是一个历史问题。

Only in our self -confident day of the popularization of knowledge — thanks to that most powerful engine of ignorance , the diffusion of printed matter — has the question of the freedom of will been put on a level on which the question itself cannot exist . In our time the majority of so -called advanced people — that is , the crowd of ignoramuses — have taken the work of the naturalists who deal with one side of the question for a solution of the whole problem .

只有在我们这个自信的知识普及时代--这要归功于最强大的无知引擎--印刷品的传播--意志自由的问题才被放到了一个问题本身不可能存在的高度。在我们这个时代，大多数所谓的先进分子，也就是一群无知者，把自然科学家研究问题的一个方面当作整个问题的解决方案。

They say and write and print that the soul and freedom do not exist , for the life of man is expressed by muscular movements and muscular movements are conditioned by the activity of the nerves ; the soul and free will do not exist because at an unknown period of time we sprang from the apes . They say this , not at all suspecting that thousands of years ago that same law of necessity which with such ardor they are now trying to prove by physiology and comparative zoology was not merely acknowledged by all the religions and all the thinkers , but has never been denied . They do not see that the role of the natural sciences in this matter is merely to serve as an instrument for the illumination of one side of it . For the fact that , from the point of view of observation , reason and the will are merely secretions of the brain , and that man following the general law may have developed from lower animals at some unknown period of time , only explains from a fresh side the truth admitted thousands of years ago by all the religious and philosophic theories — that from the point of view of reason man is subject to the law of necessity ; but it does not advance by a hair's breadth the solution of the question , which has another , opposite , side , based on the consciousness of freedom .

他们说、写、印，灵魂和自由并不存在，因为人的生命是通过肌肉运动表现出来的，而肌肉运动又受神经活动的制约；灵魂和自由意志并不存在，因为在不知道什么时候，我们从猿猴那里诞生了。他们这样说，完全没有想到，几千年前，他们现在如此热衷于试图用生理学和比较动物学来证明的同一必然规律，不仅得到了所有宗教和思想家的承认，而且从未被否定过。他们没有看到，自然科学在这个问题上的作用仅仅是作为一种工具来揭示其中的一面。因为，从观察的角度来看，理性和意志只是大脑的分泌物，而遵循一般规律的人类可能是在某个不为人知的时期从低等动物发展而来的，这一事实只是从一个全新的侧面解释了数千年前所有宗教和哲学理论所承认的真理--从理性的角度来看，人类受制于必然规律；但它并没有以毫厘之差推进这个问题的解决，因为它还有另一个相反的侧面，那就是以自由意识为基础。

If men descended from the apes at an unknown period of time , that is as comprehensible as that they were made from a handful of earth at a certain period of time (in the first case the unknown quantity is the time , in the second case it is the origin); and the question of how man's consciousness of freedom is to be reconciled with the law of necessity to which he is subject cannot be solved by comparative physiology and zoology , for in a frog , a rabbit , or an ape , we can observe only the muscular nervous activity , but in man we observe consciousness as well as the muscular and nervous activity .

如果说人是在一个未知的时间段从猿类繁衍出来的，这就如同人是在某个时间段从一把泥土中创造出来的一样容易理解（在前一种情况下，未知量是时间，在后一种情况下，未知量是起源）；在青蛙、兔子或猿猴身上，我们只能观察到肌肉和神经的活动，但在人身上，我们不仅能观察到肌肉和神经的活动，还能观察到人的意识。

The naturalists and their followers , thinking they can solve this question , are like plasterers set to plaster one side of the walls of a church who , availing themselves of the absence of the chief superintendent of the work , should in an access of zeal plaster over the windows , icons , woodwork , and still unbuttressed walls , and should be delighted that from their point of view as plasterers , everything is now so smooth and regular .

自然主义者和他们的追随者自以为可以解决这个问题，就像在教堂的墙壁上抹灰的泥水匠，趁着工程总管不在的时候，兴致勃勃地在窗户、图标、木制品和仍未开槽的墙壁上抹灰，并欣喜地认为，从他们泥水匠的角度来看，现在一切都如此光滑和规整。
