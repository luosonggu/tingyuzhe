﻿CHAPTER XV

第十五章

When Natasha opened Prince Andrew's door with a familiar movement and let Princess Mary pass into the room before her , the princess felt the sobs in her throat. Hard as she had tried to prepare herself , and now tried to remain tranquil , she knew that she would be unable to look at him without tears .

当娜塔莎用熟悉的动作打开安德鲁王子的房门，让玛丽公主走进她面前的房间时，公主感到喉咙里一阵哽咽。尽管她努力让自己做好准备，现在又努力让自己保持平静，但她知道自己将无法不流泪地看着他。

The princess understood what Natasha had meant by the words : " two days ago this suddenly happened ." She understood those words to mean that he had suddenly softened and that this softening and gentleness were signs of approaching death . As she stepped to the door she already saw in imagination Andrew's face as she remembered it in childhood , a gentle , mild , sympathetic face which he had rarely shown , and which therefore affected her very strongly . She was sure he would speak soft , tender words to her such as her father had uttered before his death , and that she would not be able to bear it and would burst into sobs in his presence . Yet sooner or later it had to be , and she went in . The sobs rose higher and higher in her throat as she more and more clearly distinguished his form and her shortsighted eyes tried to make out his features , and then she saw his face and met his gaze .

公主明白娜塔莎的话是什么意思："两天前，这件事突然发生了"她理解这些话的意思是，他突然变得软弱了，而这种软弱和温柔是死亡临近的征兆。当她走到门口时，她已经在想象中看到了安德鲁的脸，就像她童年时记忆中的一样，那是一张温柔、温和、富有同情心的脸，他很少露出这样的表情，因此对她的影响非常大。她确信他会对她说一些温柔体贴的话，就像她父亲生前对她说的那样，而她会无法忍受，在他面前泣不成声。然而迟早都要这样，于是她走了进去。当她越来越清晰地分辨出他的身形，近视的眼睛试图辨认出他的特征时，她喉咙里的呜咽声越来越高，然后她看到了他的脸，并与他对视。

He was lying in a squirrel - fur dressing gown on a divan , surrounded by pillows . He was thin and pale . In one thin , translucently white hand he held a handkerchief , while with the other he stroked the delicate mustache he had grown , moving his fingers slowly . His eyes gazed at them as they entered .

他穿着松鼠毛的睡袍，躺在沙发上，周围放满了枕头。他很瘦，脸色苍白。他一只瘦削、半透明的白手拿着一块手帕，另一只手抚摸着自己蓄起的精致的胡须，手指缓慢地移动着。当他们走进来时，他的眼睛注视着他们。

On seeing his face and meeting his eyes Princess Mary's pace suddenly slackened , she felt her tears dry up and her sobs ceased . She suddenly felt guilty and grew timid on catching the expression of his face and eyes .

看到他的脸和他的眼睛，玛丽公主的脚步突然放慢了，她感到眼泪流干了，抽泣也停止了。她突然感到内疚，在捕捉到他的表情和眼神后，她变得胆怯起来。

" But in what am I to blame ?" she asked herself . And his cold , stern look replied : " Because you are alive and thinking of the living , while I ..."

"她问自己："我有什么错呢？他冷峻的眼神回答道"因为你还活着，还想着活着的人，而我......"

In the deep gaze that seemed to look not outwards but inwards there was an almost hostile expression as he slowly regarded his sister and Natasha .

在他深邃的目光中，似乎不是向外看，而是向内看，他缓缓地看着妹妹和娜塔莎，表情近乎敌意。

He kissed his sister , holding her hand in his as was their wont .

他亲吻了妹妹，按照他们的习惯握住了她的手。

" How are you , Mary ? How did you manage to get here ?" said he in a voice as calm and aloof as his look .

"你好吗，玛丽？你是怎么来到这里的？"他的声音平静而冷漠，就像他的表情一样。

Had he screamed in agony , that scream would not have struck such horror into Princess Mary's heart as the tone of his voice .

如果他痛苦地尖叫起来，那尖叫声也不会像他的语气一样让玛丽公主感到如此恐怖。

" And have you brought little Nicholas ?" he asked in the same slow , quiet manner and with an obvious effort to remember .

"你把小尼古拉斯带来了吗？"他用同样缓慢、平静的语气问道，显然在努力回忆。

" How are you now ?" said Princess Mary , herself surprised at what she was saying .

"你现在怎么样？"玛丽公主说，她自己也对自己所说的话感到惊讶。

" That , my dear , you must ask the doctor ," he replied , and again making an evident effort to be affectionate , he said with his lips only (his words clearly did not correspond to his thoughts):

"这个，亲爱的，你得问医生，"他回答道，并再次明显地努力表现出亲切的样子，只用嘴唇说（他的话显然与他的想法不符）：

"Merci, chere amie, d'etre venue." *

"谢谢您，亲爱的女士，谢谢您来到这里。*

* " Thank you for coming , my dear ."

* "谢谢您的光临，亲爱的"。

Princess Mary pressed his hand . The pressure made him wince just perceptibly . He was silent , and she did not know what to say . She now understood what had happened to him two days before . In his words , his tone , and especially in that calm , almost antagonistic look could be felt an estrangement from everything belonging to this world, terrible in one who is alive . Evidently only with an effort did he understand anything living ; but it was obvious that he failed to understand , not because he lacked the power to do so but because he understood something else — something the living did not and could not understand — and which wholly occupied his mind .

玛丽公主按住了他的手。这一按让他微微一颤。他沉默不语，她不知道该说什么。她现在明白了两天前发生在他身上的事情。从他的话语、语气，尤其是那平静得近乎对立的眼神中，可以感受到他对这个世界上一切事物的疏远，一个活着的人的疏远是可怕的。显然，他只有努力才能理解任何有生命的东西；但很明显，他之所以不能理解，并不是因为他缺乏理解的能力，而是因为他理解了另外一些东西--一些有生命的人不理解也无法理解的东西--而这些东西完全占据了他的思想。

" There , you see how strangely fate has brought us together ," said he , breaking the silence and pointing to Natasha . " She looks after me all the time ."

"他打破沉默，指着娜塔莎说："你看，命运让我们相遇是多么奇妙。"她一直在照顾我。"

Princess Mary heard him and did not understand how he could say such a thing . He , the sensitive , tender Prince Andrew , how could he say that , before her whom he loved and who loved him ? Had he expected to live he could not have said those words in that offensively cold tone . If he had not known that he was dying , how could he have failed to pity her and how could he speak like that in her presence ? The only explanation was that he was indifferent , because something else , much more important , had been revealed to him .

玛丽公主听了他的话，不明白他怎么会说出这样的话。他，敏感、温柔的安德鲁王子，怎么能在他爱的人和爱他的人面前说出这样的话？如果他还想活下去，他不可能用那种令人不快的冷酷语气说出这些话。如果他不知道自己已经奄奄一息，又怎么会不怜惜她，又怎么会当着她的面说出那样的话呢？唯一的解释是，他无动于衷，因为有其他更重要的事情向他揭示了。

The conversation was cold and disconnected and continually broke off .

谈话冷淡而断断续续。

" Mary came by way of Ryazan," said Natasha .

"娜塔莎说："玛丽是从梁赞来的。

Prince Andrew did not notice that she called his sister Mary , and only after calling her so in his presence did Natasha notice it herself .

安德鲁王子没有注意到她称他的妹妹为玛丽，直到当着他的面这样称呼她之后，娜塔莎自己才注意到。

" Really ?" he asked .

"真的吗？"他问道。

" They told her that all Moscow has been burned down , and that ..."

"他们告诉她，整个莫斯科都被烧毁了，而且......"

Natasha stopped . It was impossible to talk . It was plain that he was making an effort to listen , but could not do so .

娜塔莎停住了。她说不出话来。很明显，他在努力倾听，但却无法做到。

" Yes , they say it's burned ," he said . " It's a great pity ," and he gazed straight before him , absently stroking his mustache with his fingers .

"是的，他们说已经烧毁了，"他说。"太可惜了。"他直直地望着眼前的一切，用手指哂笑着抚摸着自己的胡子。

" And so you have met Count Nicholas , Mary ?" Prince Andrew suddenly said , evidently wishing to speak pleasantly to them . " He wrote here that he took a great liking to you ," he went on simply and calmly , evidently unable to understand all the complex significance his words had for living people . " If you liked him too , it would be a good thing for you to get married ," he added rather more quickly , as if pleased at having found words he had long been seeking .

"你见过尼古拉斯伯爵了，玛丽？"安德鲁王子突然说道，显然是想跟他们寒暄几句。"他在信中说，他非常喜欢你，"他简单而平静地继续说道，显然无法理解他的话对活人所具有的复杂意义。"如果你也喜欢他，那么结婚对你们来说是件好事，"他语速更快地补充道，似乎为找到了他寻找已久的话而感到高兴。

Princess Mary heard his words but they had no meaning for her , except as a proof of how far away he now was from everything living .

玛丽公主听到了他的话，但这些话对她来说没有任何意义，只能证明他现在离生活中的一切是多么遥远。

" Why talk of me ?" she said quietly and glanced at Natasha .

"为什么要谈论我？"她轻声说道，并瞥了一眼娜塔莎。

Natasha , who felt her glance , did not look at her . All three were again silent.

娜塔莎感觉到了她的目光，但没有看她。三个人又都沉默了。

" Andrew , would you like ..." Princess Mary suddenly said in a trembling voice , " would you like to see little Nicholas ? He is always talking about you !"

"安德鲁，你想......"玛丽公主突然用颤抖的声音说："你想看看小尼古拉斯吗？他总是说起你！"

Prince Andrew smiled just perceptibly and for the first time , but Princess Mary , who knew his face so well , saw with horror that he did not smile with pleasure or affection for his son , but with quiet , gentle irony because he thought she was trying what she believed to be the last means of arousing him .

安德鲁王子第一次露出了明显的笑容，但玛丽公主非常了解他的表情，她惊恐地发现，他的笑容并不是因为高兴，也不是因为对儿子的爱，而是因为他认为她在尝试她认为是唤起他的最后一种手段，所以才会露出平静而温柔的讽刺。

" Yes , I shall be very glad to see him . Is he quite well ?"

"是的，我很高兴见到他。他还好吗？"

When little Nicholas was brought into Prince Andrew's room he looked at his father with frightened eyes , but did not cry , because no one else was crying . Prince Andrew kissed him and evidently did not know what to say to him .

当小尼古拉斯被带进安德鲁王子的房间时，他用惊恐的眼神看着父亲，但没有哭，因为没有其他人在哭。安德鲁王子亲吻了他，显然不知道该对他说什么。

When Nicholas had been led away , Princess Mary again went up to her brother , kissed him , and unable to restrain her tears any longer began to cry .

尼古拉斯被领走后，玛丽公主又走到哥哥身边，亲吻了他一下，再也抑制不住眼泪，开始哭了起来。

He looked at her attentively .

哥哥专注地看着她。

" Is it about Nicholas ?" he asked .

"是关于尼古拉斯的吗？"他问。

Princess Mary nodded her head , weeping .

玛丽公主点点头，哭了起来。

" Mary , you know the Gosp ..." but he broke off .

"玛丽，你知道福音书...... "但他断断续续

" What did you say ?"

"你说了什么？

" Nothing . You mustn't cry here ," he said , looking at her with the same cold expression .

"没说什么。你不能在这里哭，"他说，用同样冷漠的表情看着她。

When Princess Mary began to cry , he understood that she was crying at the thought that little Nicholas would be left without a father . With a great effort he tried to return to life and to see things from their point of view .

当玛丽公主开始哭泣时，他明白她是想到小尼古拉斯将失去父亲而哭泣。他努力让自己回到生活中来，从他们的角度看问题。

" Yes , to them it must seem sad !" he thought . " But how simple it is .

"他想，"是的，对他们来说，这一定很悲惨。"但事情其实很简单

" The fowls of the air sow not , neither do they reap , yet your Father feedeth them ," he said to himself and wished to say to Princess Mary ; " but no , they will take it their own way , they won't understand ! They can't understand that all those feelings they prize so — all our feelings , all those ideas that seem so important to us , are unnecessary . We cannot understand one another ," and he remained silent .

"空中的飞鸟种也不是，收也不是，你们的父却养活它们，"他对自己说，也想对玛丽公主说；"但是不行，他们会按自己的方式行事，他们不会理解的！他们不明白，他们所珍视的那些感情--我们所有的感情，那些对我们来说似乎很重要的想法，都是多余的。我们无法相互理解。"他保持沉默。

Prince Andrew's little son was seven . He could scarcely read , and knew nothing . After that day he lived through many things , gaining knowledge , observation , and experience , but had he possessed all the faculties he afterwards acquired , he could not have had a better or more profound understanding of the meaning of the scene he had witnessed between his father , Mary , and Natasha , than he had then . He understood it completely , and , leaving the room without crying , went silently up to Natasha who had come out with him and looked shyly at her with his beautiful , thoughtful eyes , then his uplifted , rosy upper lip trembled and leaning his head against her he began to cry .

安德鲁王子的小儿子七岁了。他几乎不识字，什么也不懂。那天以后，他经历了许多事情，获得了知识、观察力和经验，但如果他拥有后来获得的所有能力，他也不可能比当时更好、更深刻地理解他目睹的父亲、玛丽和娜塔莎之间那一幕的含义。他完全明白了，没有哭，默默地离开了房间，走到和他一起出来的娜塔莎身边，用他那双美丽而深思的眼睛羞涩地望着她，然后他那高高扬起的、红润的上唇颤抖了一下，把头靠在她的身上，开始哭了起来。

After that he avoided Dessalles and the countess who caressed him and either sat alone or came timidly to Princess Mary , or to Natasha of whom he seemed even fonder than of his aunt , and clung to them quietly and shyly .

此后，他躲开了德萨雷斯和爱抚他的伯爵夫人，要么独自坐着，要么怯生生地来到玛丽公主或娜塔莎身边，他似乎比姨妈还要喜欢娜塔莎，悄悄地、害羞地依偎在她们身边。

When Princess Mary had left Prince Andrew she fully understood what Natasha's face had told her . She did not speak any more to Natasha of hopes of saving his life . She took turns with her beside his sofa , and did not cry any more , but prayed continually , turning in soul to that Eternal and Unfathomable , whose presence above the dying man was now so evident .

玛丽公主离开安德鲁王子后，完全明白娜塔莎的表情告诉了她什么。她没有再对娜塔莎说救他一命的希望。她轮流陪着娜塔莎坐在安德鲁王子的沙发旁，不再哭泣，而是不停地祈祷，在灵魂深处向那位永恒而深不可测的神灵求助。
