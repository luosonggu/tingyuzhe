﻿CHAPTER IV

第四章

It is natural for us who were not living in those days to imagine that when half Russia had been conquered and the inhabitants were fleeing to distant provinces , and one levy after another was being raised for the defense of the fatherland , all Russians from the greatest to the least were solely engaged in sacrificing themselves , saving their fatherland , or weeping over its downfall . The tales and descriptions of that time without exception speak only of the self - sacrifice , patriotic devotion , despair , grief , and the heroism of the Russians . But it was not really so . It appears so to us because we see only the general historic interest of that time and do not see all the personal human interests that people had . Yet in reality those personal interests of the moment so much transcend the general interests that they always prevent the public interest from being felt or even noticed . Most of the people at that time paid no attention to the general progress of events but were guided only by their private interests , and they were the very people whose activities at that period were most useful .

我们这些没有生活在那个时代的人很自然会想象，当半个俄罗斯被征服，居民逃往遥远的省份，为保卫祖国而征收的税款一个接着一个，所有的俄罗斯人，从最伟大的到最卑微的，都在为牺牲自己、拯救祖国或为祖国的灭亡而哭泣。关于那个时代的传说和描述无一例外地只谈到俄罗斯人的自我牺牲、爱国奉献、绝望、悲痛和英雄主义。但事实并非如此。我们之所以这样认为，是因为我们只看到了当时的普遍历史利益，而没有看到人们的所有个人利益。但实际上，当时的个人利益远远超越了普遍利益，以至于总是让人无法感受到甚至无法注意到公共利益。当时的大多数人并不关注事件的总体进展，而只是以他们的个人利益为导向，而他们正是那个时期活动最有用的人。

Those who tried to understand the general course of events and to take part in it by self - sacrifice and heroism were the most useless members of society , they saw everything upside down , and all they did for the common good turned out to be useless and foolish — like Pierre's and Mamonov's regiments which looted Russian villages , and the lint the young ladies prepared and that never reached the wounded , and so on . Even those , fond of intellectual talk and of expressing their feelings , who discussed Russia's position at the time involuntarily introduced into their conversation either a shade of pretense and falsehood or useless condemnation and anger directed against people accused of actions no one could possibly be guilty of . In historic events the rule forbidding us to eat of the fruit of the Tree of Knowledge is specially applicable . Only unconscious action bears fruit , and he who plays a part in an historic event never understands its significance . If he tries to realize it his efforts are fruitless .

那些试图了解事件的总体进程并以自我牺牲和英雄主义参与其中的人是社会中最无用的人，他们把一切都看得颠三倒四，他们为共同利益所做的一切都变成了无用和愚蠢的事，就像皮埃尔和马蒙诺夫的兵团抢劫俄国村庄，年轻姑娘们准备的棉絮从未送到伤员手中，等等。即使是那些喜欢谈天说地和抒发感情的人，在讨论俄国当时的处境时，也会不由自主地在谈话中加入一些伪装和虚假的影子，或者是对那些被指控犯有任何人都不可能犯的罪行的人进行无用的谴责和愤怒。在历史事件中，禁止我们吃 "知识之树 "果实的规则特别适用。只有无意识的行动才会结出果实，而在历史事件中扮演角色的人永远不会理解其意义。如果他试图意识到这一点，他的努力也是徒劳的。

The more closely a man was engaged in the events then taking place in Russia the less did he realize their significance . In Petersburg and in the provinces at a distance from Moscow , ladies , and gentlemen in militia uniforms , wept for Russia and its ancient capital and talked of self - sacrifice and so on ; but in the army which retired beyond Moscow there was little talk or thought of Moscow , and when they caught sight of its burned ruins no one swore to be avenged on the French , but they thought about their next pay , their next quarters , of Matreshka the vivandiere , and like matters .

一个人对当时俄国发生的事件参与得越多，他就越不了解这些事件的意义。在彼得堡和距离莫斯科较远的外省，女士们和穿着民兵制服的先生们为俄国和它的古都哭泣，谈论着自我牺牲等等；但在退到莫斯科以外的军队中，却很少有人谈论或想到莫斯科，当他们看到莫斯科被烧毁的废墟时，没有人发誓要向法国人复仇，他们只是想着下一次的军饷、下一个住处、马特莱什卡（Matreshka the vivandiere）以及类似的事情。

As the war had caught him in the service , Nicholas Rostov took a close and prolonged part in the defense of his country , but did so casually , without any aim at self - sacrifice , and he therefore looked at what was going on in Russia without despair and without dismally racking his brains over it . Had he been asked what he thought of the state of Russia , he would have said that it was not his business to think about it , that Kutuzov and others were there for that purpose , but that he had heard that the regiments were to be made up to their full strength , that fighting would probably go on for a long time yet , and that things being so it was quite likely he might be in command of a regiment in a couple of years ' time .

尼古拉-罗斯托夫是在战争中服役的，因此他参加了保卫祖国的战斗，但他只是随随便便地参加了战斗，并没有任何自我牺牲的目的，因此他对俄国发生的一切并不感到绝望，也没有为此而沮丧地绞尽脑汁。如果有人问他对俄国的现状有什么看法，他会说这不是他该考虑的事，库图佐夫和其他人就是为此目的而去的，但他听说各团都将满员，战斗可能还会持续很长时间，既然如此，他很有可能在几年后就能指挥一个团。

As he looked at the matter in this way , he learned that he was being sent to Voronezh to buy remounts for his division , not only without regret at being prevented from taking part in the coming battle , but with the greatest pleasure — which he did not conceal and which his comrades fully understood .

当他这样看问题时，他得知自己被派往沃罗涅日为他的师购买马匹，他不仅没有因为不能参加即将到来的战斗而感到遗憾，反而非常高兴--他没有隐瞒这一点，他的战友们也完全理解他的心情。

A few days before the battle of Borodino , Nicholas received the necessary money and warrants , and having sent some hussars on in advance , he set out with post horses for Voronezh .

在博罗季诺战役的前几天，尼古拉收到了所需的钱和军令状，他先派了一些轻骑兵，带着驿马出发前往沃罗涅日。

Only a man who has experienced it — that is , has passed some months continuously in an atmosphere of campaigning and war — can understand the delight Nicholas felt when he escaped from the region covered by the army's foraging operations , provision trains , and hospitals . When — free from soldiers , wagons, and the filthy traces of a camp—he saw villages with peasants and peasant women , gentlemen's country houses , fields where cattle were grazing , posthouses with stationmasters asleep in them , he rejoiced as though seeing all this for the first time . What for a long while specially surprised and delighted him were the women , young and healthy , without a dozen officers making up to each of them ; women , too , who were pleased and flattered that a passing officer should joke with them .

只有亲身经历过的人，也就是在战役和战争气氛中连续度过几个月的人，才能理解尼古拉从军队的觅食行动、辎重列车和医院所覆盖的地区逃出来时的喜悦心情。当他摆脱了士兵、马车和军营的污秽痕迹，看到有农民和农妇的村庄、绅士的乡间别墅、放牧的田野、站长在里面睡觉的邮局时，他欣喜若狂，仿佛第一次看到这一切。尤其让他感到惊讶和欣喜的是那些妇女，她们年轻而健康，没有十几个军官和她们打成一片；还有那些妇女，她们对一个路过的军官和她们开玩笑感到高兴和受宠若惊。

In the highest spirits Nicholas arrived at night at a hotel in Voronezh , ordered things he had long been deprived of in camp , and next day , very clean -shaven and in a full - dress uniform he had not worn for a long time , went to present himself to the authorities .

尼古拉兴致勃勃地连夜赶到沃罗涅日的一家旅馆，订购了他在军营中久违的东西，第二天，他刮得干干净净，穿着很久没有穿过的全套军装，去向当局自报家门。

The commander of the militia was a civilian general , an old man who was evidently pleased with his military designation and rank . He received Nicholas brusquely (imagining this to be characteristically military ) and questioned him with an important air , as if considering the general progress of affairs and approving and disapproving with full right to do so . Nicholas was in such good spirits that this merely amused him .

民兵指挥官是一位文职将军，他是一位老人，显然对自己的军衔和军衔很满意。他粗暴地接待了尼古拉斯（想象这是军人的特点），并以一种重要的口吻询问他，似乎在考虑事务的总体进展情况，并完全有权这样做，批准或不批准。尼古拉的心情非常好，这只是逗他开心而已。

From the commander of the militia he drove to the governor . The governor was a brisk little man , very simple and affable . He indicated the stud farms at which Nicholas might procure horses , recommended to him a horse dealer in the town and a landowner fourteen miles out of town who had the best horses , and promised to assist him in every way .

从民兵指挥官那里出来，他驱车前往州长那里。总督是个爽快的小个子，非常憨厚和蔼。他指出了尼古拉斯可以购买马匹的马场，向他推荐了城里的一个马商和城外十四英里处的一个拥有最好马匹的地主，并答应在各方面为他提供帮助。

" You are Count Ilya Rostov's son ? My wife was a great friend of your mother's . We are at home on Thursdays — today is Thursday , so please come and see us quite informally ," said the governor , taking leave of him .

"你是伊利亚-罗斯托夫伯爵的儿子？我妻子是你母亲的好朋友。我们周四都在家--今天就是周四，所以请您来见我们，我们很随意的。"州长告辞说。

Immediately on leaving the governor's , Nicholas hired post horses and , taking his squadron quartermaster with him , drove at a gallop to the landowner , fourteen miles away , who had the stud . Everything seemed to him pleasant and easy during that first part of his stay in Voronezh and , as usually happens when a man is in a pleasant state of mind , everything went well and easily .

离开总督府后，尼古拉斯立即雇了驿马，带着他的中队军需官，驱车疾驰到十四英里外的地主家，那里有他的种马。在沃罗涅日逗留的最初一段时间里，尼古拉觉得一切都很愉快和轻松，就像一个人心情愉快时通常会发生的那样，一切都很顺利和轻松。

The landowner to whom Nicholas went was a bachelor , an old cavalryman , a horse fancier , a sportsman , the possessor of some century - old brandy and some old Hungarian wine , who had a snuggery where he smoked , and who owned some splendid horses .

尼古拉斯去找的地主是个单身汉，一个老骑兵，一个马迷，一个运动健将，拥有一些百年白兰地和匈牙利老酒，他有一个可以抽烟的小屋子，还拥有几匹漂亮的马。

In very few words Nicholas bought seventeen picked stallions for six thousand rubles — to serve , as he said , as samples of his remounts . After dining and taking rather too much of the Hungarian wine , Nicholas — having exchanged kisses with the landowner , with whom he was already on the friendliest terms — galloped back over abominable roads , in the brightest frame of mind , continually urging on the driver so as to be in time for the governor's party .

尼古拉斯三言两语就用六千卢布买下了十七匹精挑细选的种马，他说这些马可以作为他的改装马的样品。用餐后，尼古拉斯喝了太多的匈牙利葡萄酒，在与地主交换了亲吻后（他与地主的关系已经是最友好的了），他在可恶的道路上疾驰而回，心情非常愉快，不断催促车夫，以便及时赶上州长的聚会。

When he had changed , poured water over his head , and scented himself , Nicholas arrived at the governor's rather late , but with the phrase " better late than never " on his lips .

尼古拉斯换好衣服，往头上浇了点水，又给自己喷了点香水，这才姗姗来迟，但他嘴上却说着 "迟到总比不到好"。

It was not a ball , nor had dancing been announced , but everyone knew that Catherine Petrovna would play valses and the ecossaise on the clavichord and that there would be dancing , and so everyone had come as to a ball .

这不是一场舞会，也没有宣布要跳舞，但大家都知道凯瑟琳-彼得罗夫娜会用克拉维奇琴演奏瓦尔塞舞曲和埃科萨舞曲，而且还会跳舞，所以大家都像参加舞会一样赶来了。

Provincial life in 1812 went on very much as usual , but with this difference , that it was livelier in the towns in consequence of the arrival of many wealthy families from Moscow , and as in everything that went on in Russia at that time a special recklessness was noticeable , an " in for a penny , in for a pound — who cares ?" spirit , and the inevitable small talk , instead of turning on the weather and mutual acquaintances , now turned on Moscow , the army , and Napoleon .

1812 年的省城生活和往常一样，但有一点不同，那就是由于许多富裕家庭从莫斯科赶来，城镇里的生活更加热闹了，而且在当时俄国的一切活动中，人们都会发现一种特别的鲁莽，一种 "为一分钱，为一磅，谁在乎？

The society gathered together at the governor's was the best in Voronezh .

在总督府聚集的社交圈是沃罗涅日最好的。

There were a great many ladies and some of Nicholas ' Moscow acquaintances , but there were no men who could at all vie with the cavalier of St . George , the hussar remount officer , the good - natured and well - bred Count Rostov . Among the men was an Italian prisoner , an officer of the French army ; and Nicholas felt that the presence of that prisoner enhanced his own importance as a Russian hero . The Italian was , as it were , a war trophy . Nicholas felt this , it seemed to him that everyone regarded the Italian in the same light , and he treated him cordially though with dignity and restraint .

这里有许多女士和尼古拉在莫斯科的一些熟人，但却没有一个男人能与圣乔治骑兵、轻骑兵军官、好脾气又有教养的罗斯托夫伯爵相提并论。这些人中有一名意大利俘虏，他是法国军队的一名军官；尼古拉觉得，这名俘虏的出现提高了他作为俄罗斯英雄的重要性。这个意大利人可以说是战利品。尼古拉感受到了这一点，在他看来，每个人都以同样的眼光看待这个意大利人，因此他热情地款待了他，但同时也保持了尊严和克制。

As soon as Nicholas entered in his hussar uniform , diffusing around him a fragrance of perfume and wine , and had uttered the words " better late than never " and heard them repeated several times by others , people clustered around him ; all eyes turned on him , and he felt at once that he had entered into his proper position in the province — that of a universal favorite : a very pleasant position , and intoxicatingly so after his long privations . At posting stations , at inns , and in the landowner's snuggery , maidservants had been flattered by his notice , and here too at the governor's party there were ( as it seemed to Nicholas ) an inexhaustible number of pretty young women , married and unmarried , impatiently awaiting his notice . The women and girls flirted with him and , from the first day , the people concerned themselves to get this fine young daredevil of an hussar married and settled down . Among these was the governor's wife herself , who welcomed Rostov as a near relative and called him " Nicholas ."

尼古拉一穿上轻骑兵制服，周身散发着香水和葡萄酒的芬芳，一说到 "迟到总比不到好 "这句话，并听到别人重复了好几遍，人们就簇拥着他；所有的目光都投向了他，他立刻感到自己在这个省已经占据了应有的位置--一个人人都喜欢的位置：一个非常令人愉快的位置，在长期的匮乏之后，这样的位置令人陶醉。在驿站、旅店和地主的小屋里，女仆们都对他的到来感到受宠若惊，而在总督的宴会上，也有（尼古拉斯看来）数不胜数的年轻漂亮的女人，不管是已婚的还是未婚的，都迫不及待地等待着他的到来。女人们和姑娘们都在和他调情，从第一天起，人们就开始关心如何让这个年轻有为的轻骑兵结婚安家。其中就包括总督夫人本人，她把罗斯托夫当作近亲欢迎，并称他为 "尼古拉斯"。

Catherine Petrovna did actually play valses and the ecossaise , and dancing began in which Nicholas still further captivated the provincial society by his agility . His particularly free manner of dancing even surprised them all . Nicholas was himself rather surprised at the way he danced that evening . He had never danced like that in Moscow and would even have considered such a very free and easy manner improper and in bad form , but here he felt it incumbent on him to astonish them all by something unusual , something they would have to accept as the regular thing in the capital though new to them in the provinces .

凯瑟琳-彼得罗夫娜还真的弹奏了瓦尔斯舞曲和埃科塞斯舞曲，尼古拉斯也开始跳舞，他的敏捷进一步吸引了外省人。他那特别自由的舞姿甚至让所有人都大吃一惊。尼古拉斯本人也对自己当晚的舞姿感到惊讶。他在莫斯科从来没有跳过这样的舞，甚至会认为这种非常自由自在的方式是不恰当的，也是不正规的，但在这里，他觉得自己有责任用不寻常的方式让他们所有人都感到惊讶，虽然在外省，他们对这种方式感到很新奇，但在首都，他们不得不把这种方式当作常规来接受。

All the evening Nicholas paid attention to a blue - eyed , plump and pleasing little blonde , the wife of one of the provincial officials . With the naive conviction of young men in a merry mood that other men's wives were created for them , Rostov did not leave the lady's side and treated her husband in a friendly and conspiratorial style , as if , without speaking of it , they knew how capitally Nicholas and the lady would get on together . The husband , however , did not seem to share that conviction and tried to behave morosely with Rostov . But the latter's good - natured naivete was so boundless that sometimes even he involuntarily yielded to Nicholas ' good humor . Toward the end of the evening , however , as the wife's face grew more flushed and animated , the husband's became more and more melancholy and solemn , as though there were but a given amount of animation between them and as the wife's share increased the husband's diminished .

整个晚上，尼古拉斯都在关注一个蓝眼睛、丰满、讨人喜欢的金发小姑娘，她是省里一位官员的妻子。罗斯托夫天真地以为别人的妻子都是为自己量身定做的，他没有离开这位女士的身边，并以友好和阴谋的方式对待她的丈夫，仿佛不用说，他们也知道尼古拉斯和这位女士会相处得多么融洽。然而，丈夫似乎并不同意这种看法，他试图对罗斯托夫表现得很冷淡。但后者的天真善良是如此无边无际，以至于有时连他自己也不由自主地屈服于尼古拉的幽默。然而，晚会快结束时，随着妻子的脸色越来越红润、越来越生动，丈夫的脸色却越来越忧郁、越来越庄重，好像他们之间只有一定的生动性，妻子的生动性增加了，丈夫的生动性就减少了。
