﻿CHAPTER XVIII

第十八章

Prince Bagration , having reached the highest point of our right flank , began riding downhill to where the roll of musketry was heard but where on account of the smoke nothing could be seen . The nearer they got to the hollow the less they could see but the more they felt the nearness of the actual battlefield . They began to meet wounded men . One with a bleeding head and no cap was being dragged along by two soldiers who supported him under the arms . There was a gurgle in his throat and he was spitting blood . A bullet had evidently hit him in the throat or mouth . Another was walking sturdily by himself but without his musket , groaning aloud and swinging his arm which had just been hurt , while blood from it was streaming over his greatcoat as from a bottle . He had that moment been wounded and his face showed fear rather than suffering . Crossing a road they descended a steep incline and saw several men lying on the ground ; they also met a crowd of soldiers some of whom were unwounded . The soldiers were ascending the hill breathing heavily , and despite the general's presence were talking loudly and gesticulating . In front of them rows of gray cloaks were already visible through the smoke , and an officer catching sight of Bagration rushed shouting after the crowd of retreating soldiers , ordering them back . Bagration rode up to the ranks along which shots crackled now here and now there , drowning the sound of voices and the shouts of command . The whole air reeked with smoke . The excited faces of the soldiers were blackened with it . Some were using their ramrods , others putting powder on the touchpans or taking charges from their pouches , while others were firing , though who they were firing at could not be seen for the smoke which there was no wind to carry away . A pleasant humming and whistling of bullets were often heard . " What is this ?" thought Prince Andrew approaching the crowd of soldiers . " It can't be an attack , for they are not moving ; it can't be a square — for they are not drawn up for that ."

巴格拉季昂王子到达我们右翼的最高点后，开始骑马下山，到了能听到滚滚火枪声但由于硝烟弥漫什么也看不见的地方。他们越靠近山凹，看到的东西就越少，但却越发感到战场的逼近。他们开始遇到伤员。一名头部流血、没有戴帽子的伤员被两名士兵搀扶着拖着走。他的喉咙里发出咯咯的声音，还在吐血。显然，一颗子弹击中了他的喉咙或嘴巴。另一个人独自结结实实地走着，但没有带火枪，他大声呻吟着，摆动着刚刚受伤的手臂，鲜血像瓶子里的血一样流满了他的大衣。他刚才受了伤，脸上没有痛苦，反而流露出恐惧。穿过一条路，他们下了一个陡坡，看到几个人躺在地上；他们还遇到了一群士兵，其中有些人没有受伤。士兵们喘着粗气爬上山坡，尽管将军在场，他们还是大声说话，比划着手势。在他们前面，一排排灰色的斗篷已经透过烟雾清晰可见，一名军官看到了巴格拉季昂，急忙大喊着追赶撤退的士兵，命令他们返回。巴格拉季昂骑马冲到队伍前，枪声此起彼伏，淹没了人声和喊话声。整个空气中弥漫着硝烟的味道。士兵们兴奋的脸都被烟熏黑了。有的在使用撞锤，有的在往火药桶里放火药，有的在从火药袋里拿火药，还有的在射击，虽然看不见他们在向谁射击，但因为没有风，烟雾无法散去。经常能听到子弹发出的悦耳的嗡嗡声和呼啸声。"这是什么？"安德鲁王子走近士兵人群想。"不可能是进攻，因为他们没有移动；不可能是方阵，因为他们没有为此拉开阵势。

The commander of the regiment , a thin , feeble - looking old man with a pleasant smile — his eyelids drooping more than half over his old eyes , giving him a mild expression , rode up to Bagration and welcomed him as a host welcomes an honored guest . He reported that his regiment had been attacked by French cavalry and that , though the attack had been repulsed , he had lost more than half his men . He said the attack had been repulsed , employing this military term to describe what had occurred to his regiment , but in reality he did not himself know what had happened during that half - hour to the troops entrusted to him , and could not say with certainty whether the attack had been repulsed or his regiment had been broken up . All he knew was that at the commencement of the action balls and shells began flying all over his regiment and hitting men and that afterwards someone had shouted " Cavalry !" and our men had begun firing . They were still firing , not at the cavalry which had disappeared , but at French infantry who had come into the hollow and were firing at our men . Prince Bagration bowed his head as a sign that this was exactly what he had desired and expected . Turning to his adjutant he ordered him to bring down the two battalions of the Sixth Chasseurs whom they had just passed . Prince Andrew was struck by the changed expression on Prince Bagration's face at this moment . It expressed the concentrated and happy resolution you see on the face of a man who on a hot day takes a final run before plunging into the water . The dull , sleepy expression was no longer there , nor the affectation of profound thought . The round , steady , hawk's eyes looked before him eagerly and rather disdainfully , not resting on anything although his movements were still slow and measured .

团长是个看起来瘦弱的老人，笑容可掬--他的眼皮耷拉了一半多，遮住了他的老眼，给人一种温和的感觉，他骑马来到巴格拉季昂面前，像主人欢迎贵宾一样欢迎他。他报告说，他所在的团遭到了法国骑兵的袭击，虽然袭击被击退，但他损失了一半以上的士兵。他说进攻被击退了，用了这个军事术语来描述他的团所发生的情况，但实际上他自己也不知道托付给他的部队在那半小时里发生了什么，也不能肯定地说进攻是被击退了还是他的团被打散了。他只知道在战斗开始时，子弹和炮弹开始在他的团里飞来飞去，打在人身上，后来有人喊了一声 "骑兵！"，我们的人就开始射击。他们仍在射击，但不是向已经消失的骑兵射击，而是向冲进空地并向我们的士兵射击的法国步兵射击。巴格拉季昂王子低下头，表示这正是他所希望和期待的。他转向副官，命令他把刚刚经过的第六夏瑟尔部队的两个营带下来。安德鲁王子被巴格拉季昂亲王此刻脸上变化了的表情所震撼。他的表情就像一个人在炎热的天气里，在跳入水中之前做最后的冲刺时所表现出的专注和快乐。沉闷、困倦的表情不见了，深思熟虑的表情也不见了。那双圆圆的、沉稳的鹰眼热切而又颇为不屑地看着他，虽然他的动作仍然缓慢而有分寸，但并没有停留在任何东西上。

The commander of the regiment turned to Prince Bagration , entreating him to go back as it was too dangerous to remain where they were . " Please , your excellency , for God's sake !" he kept saying , glancing for support at an officer of the suite who turned away from him . " There , you see !" and he drew attention to the bullets whistling , singing , and hissing continually around them . He spoke in the tone of entreaty and reproach that a carpenter uses to a gentleman who has picked up an ax : " We are used to it , but you , sir , will blister your hands ." He spoke as if those bullets could not kill him , and his half - closed eyes gave still more persuasiveness to his words . The staff officer joined in the colonel's appeals , but Bagration did not reply ; he only gave an order to cease firing and re - form , so as to give room for the two approaching battalions . While he was speaking , the curtain of smoke that had concealed the hollow , driven by a rising wind , began to move from right to left as if drawn by an invisible hand , and the hill opposite , with the French moving about on it , opened out before them . All eyes fastened involuntarily on this French column advancing against them and winding down over the uneven ground . One could already see the soldiers ' shaggy caps , distinguish the officers from the men , and see the standard flapping against its staff .

团长转向巴格拉季昂王子，请求他回去，因为留在原地太危险了。"求求您，阁下，看在上帝的份上！"他不停地说着，同时瞥了一眼从他身边转过身去的一名军官，以寻求支持。"在那里，你们看！"他提请大家注意，子弹在他们周围呼啸着、歌唱着，不断发出嘶嘶声。他用木匠对拿起斧头的绅士所使用的恳求和责备的语气说道："我们已经习惯了，但你，先生，你的手会起泡的"。他说得好像那些子弹不会要了他的命似的，半闭着的眼睛使他的话更有说服力。参谋也加入了上校的呼吁，但巴格拉季昂没有回答；他只是下令停止射击，重新整队，以便给两个靠近的营腾出空间。就在他说话的时候，掩盖山凹的烟幕在一阵风的推动下，开始从右向左移动，就像被一只无形的手牵引着一样，对面的山丘在法军的移动下，在他们面前豁然开朗。所有人的目光都不由自主地紧盯着这支迎面而来、在凹凸不平的地面上蜿蜒而下的法军纵队。人们已经能看到士兵们毛茸茸的帽子，能分辨出军官和士兵，还能看到旗帜在旗杆上飘扬。

" They march splendidly ," remarked someone in Bagration's suite .

"巴格拉季昂的套间里有人说："他们行进得很好。

The head of the column had already descended into the hollow . The clash would take place on this side of it ...

纵队的头已经进入了山谷。冲突将在这边发生

The remains of our regiment which had been in action rapidly formed up and moved to the right ; from behind it , dispersing the laggards , came two battalions of the Sixth Chasseurs in fine order . Before they had reached Bagration , the weighty tread of the mass of men marching in step could be heard . On their left flank , nearest to Bagration , marched a company commander , a fine round - faced man , with a stupid and happy expression — the same man who had rushed out of the wattle shed . At that moment he was clearly thinking of nothing but how dashing a fellow he would appear as he passed the commander .

我们团参加战斗的残部迅速整队，向右侧移动；第六夏瑟尔团的两个营整齐划一地从后面赶来，驱散了落伍者。在他们到达巴格拉季昂之前，就能听到大批士兵迈着整齐步伐的沉重脚步声。在他们的左翼，离巴格拉季昂最近的地方，走来了一位连长，他是个圆脸汉子，表情傻乎乎的，很高兴--他就是那个从荆条棚里冲出来的人。那一刻，他显然什么也没想，只想着在经过连长身边时，自己会显得多么潇洒。

With the self - satisfaction of a man on parade , he stepped lightly with his muscular legs as if sailing along , stretching himself to his full height without the smallest effort , his ease contrasting with the heavy tread of the soldiers who were keeping step with him . He carried close to his leg a narrow unsheathed sword (small, curved, and not like a real weapon) and looked now at the superior officers and now back at the men without losing step , his whole powerful body turning flexibly . It was as if all the powers of his soul were concentrated on passing the commander in the best possible manner , and feeling that he was doing it well he was happy . " Left ... left ... left ..." he seemed to repeat to himself at each alternate step ; and in time to this , with stern but varied faces , the wall of soldiers burdened with knapsacks and muskets marched in step , and each one of these hundreds of soldiers seemed to be repeating to himself at each alternate step , "Left... left ... left ..." A fat major skirted a bush , puffing and falling out of step ; a soldier who had fallen behind , his face showing alarm at his defection , ran at a trot , panting to catch up with his company . A cannon ball , cleaving the air , flew over the heads of Bagration and his suite , and fell into the column to the measure of " Left ... left !" " Close up !" came the company commander's voice in jaunty tones . The soldiers passed in a semicircle round something where the ball had fallen , and an old trooper on the flank , a noncommissioned officer who had stopped beside the dead men , ran to catch up his line and , falling into step with a hop , looked back angrily , and through the ominous silence and the regular tramp of feet beating the ground in unison , one seemed to hear left... left ... left .

他带着阅兵式上的自我满足感，迈着肌肉发达的双腿轻盈地走着，就像在航行一样，不费吹灰之力就把自己的身高拉到了最高，他的轻松与跟在他后面的士兵沉重的脚步形成了鲜明的对比。他的腿上紧紧挎着一把未出鞘的窄剑（小巧、弯曲，不像真正的武器），时而看看上级军官，时而回头看看士兵，脚步丝毫不减，整个有力的身体灵活地转动着。仿佛他灵魂深处所有的力量都集中在以最好的方式通过指挥官，感觉自己做得很好，他很高兴。"左......左......左...... "他似乎每隔一步都在自言自语地重复着这句话，与此同时，背着背包和火枪的士兵们也面色凝重但表情各异地迈着步子，这数百名士兵似乎每隔一步都在自言自语地重复着："左......左......左......"一个胖胖的少校绕过灌木丛，喘着粗气，步子不由自主地乱了；一个落在后面的士兵，脸上露出惊慌失措的神情，小跑着，气喘吁吁地追上自己的连队。一颗炮弹划破空气，从巴格拉季昂及其随从的头顶飞过，在 "左......左！"的枪声中落入纵队。"收队！"连长用轻快的语调喊道。士兵们绕着球掉落的地方转了半圈，侧翼的一个老兵，一个在死人旁边停下来的军士，跑过来赶上他的队伍，一跳一跳地迈开步子，愤怒地回过头去，透过不祥的寂静和脚步齐齐敲打地面的有规律的踏步声，人们似乎听到了左......左......左的声音。

" Well done , lads !" said Prince Bagration .

"干得好，小伙子们！"巴格拉季昂王子说。

" Glad to do our best , your ex'len-lency!" came a confused shout from the ranks. A morose soldier marching on the left turned his eyes on Bagration as he shouted , with an expression that seemed to say : " We know that ourselves !" Another , without looking round , as though fearing to relax , shouted with his mouth wide open and passed on .

"乐意效劳，阁下！"队伍中传来一阵混乱的喊声。在巴格拉季昂喊话的同时，左侧行军的一名士气低落的士兵将目光投向了他，表情似乎在说："我们自己也知道！"我们自己也知道！"另一名士兵没有回头看，似乎害怕放松警惕，张大嘴巴喊了一声，然后继续前进。

The order was given to halt and down knapsacks .

停止前进、放下背包的命令下达了。

Bagration rode round the ranks that had marched past him and dismounted . He gave the reins to a Cossack , took off and handed over his felt coat , stretched his legs , and set his cap straight . The head of the French column , with its officers leading , appeared from below the hill .

巴格拉季昂骑马绕过从他身边走过的队伍，下了马。他把缰绳交给一个哥萨克人，脱下并递上毡衣，伸了个懒腰，把帽子戴正。法军纵队的首领在军官的带领下，从山下出现了。

" Forward , with God !" said Bagration , in a resolute , sonorous voice , turning for a moment to the front line , and slightly swinging his arms , he went forward uneasily over the rough field with the awkward gait of a cavalryman . Prince Andrew felt that an invisible power was leading him forward , and experienced great happiness .

"前进，与上帝同在！"巴格拉季昂用坚定、铿锵的声音说道，他转身向前线走了一会儿，微微摆动手臂，在崎岖的田野上以骑兵笨拙的步态不安地向前走去。安德鲁王子感到有一种无形的力量在引领着他前进，他感到非常高兴。

The French were already near . Prince Andrew , walking beside Bagration , could clearly distinguish their bandoliers , red epaulets , and even their faces . (He distinctly saw an old French officer who , with gaitered legs and turned-out toes, climbed the hill with difficulty.) Prince Bagration gave no further orders and silently continued to walk on in front of the ranks . Suddenly one shot after another rang out from the French , smoke appeared all along their uneven ranks , and musket shots sounded . Several of our men fell , among them the round - faced officer who had marched so gaily and complacently . But at the moment the first report was heard , Bagration looked round and shouted , "Hurrah!"

法军已经近在咫尺。走在巴格拉季昂身旁的安德鲁王子可以清楚地分辨出他们的臂章、红色肩章，甚至他们的脸。(他清楚地看到一位年迈的法国军官迈着蹒跚的双腿，脚趾外翻，艰难地爬上山坡）。巴格拉季昂亲王没有再下达命令，默默地继续走在队伍前面。突然，法军的枪声接二连三地响起，他们参差不齐的队伍里冒出了滚滚浓烟，火枪声响成一片。我们有几个人倒下了，其中就有那位圆脸军官，他走得如此欢快和得意。但就在听到第一声枪响的那一刻 巴格拉季昂环顾四周 大声喊道："万岁！"

" Hurrah —ah!—ah!" rang a long- drawn shout from our ranks , and passing Bagration and racing one another they rushed in an irregular but joyous and eager crowd down the hill at their disordered foe .

"万岁--啊！--啊！"我们的队伍中响起了悠长的喊声，他们越过巴格拉季昂，互相竞跑着，以不规则但欢快急切的人群冲下山坡，扑向他们混乱的敌人。
