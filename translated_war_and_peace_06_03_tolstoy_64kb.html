﻿CHAPTER III

第三章

Next morning , having taken leave of no one but the count , and not waiting for the ladies to appear , Prince Andrew set off for home .

第二天一早，除了伯爵，安德鲁王子没有向任何人告辞，也没有等女士们出现，就启程回家了。

It was already the beginning of June when on his return journey he drove into the birch forest where the gnarled old oak had made so strange and memorable an impression on him . In the forest the harness bells sounded yet more muffled than they had done six weeks before , for now all was thick , shady , and dense , and the young firs dotted about in the forest did not jar on the general beauty but , lending themselves to the mood around , were delicately green with fluffy young shoots .

当他踏上归途，驶入那片桦树林时，已经是六月初了，那棵参天的老橡树曾给他留下了奇特而难忘的印象。在森林里，马具铃声比六个星期前更加低沉，因为现在一切都变得茂密、阴凉和浓郁，森林里点缀着的小枞树并没有破坏整体的美感，而是与周围的气氛融为一体，长出了毛茸茸的嫩芽，显得格外娇嫩。

The whole day had been hot . Somewhere a storm was gathering , but only a small cloud had scattered some raindrops lightly , sprinkling the road and the sappy leaves . The left side of the forest was dark in the shade , the right side glittered in the sunlight , wet and shiny and scarcely swayed by the breeze . Everything was in blossom , the nightingales trilled , and their voices reverberated now near , now far away .

一整天都很炎热。某处正聚集着暴风雨，但只有一小片云彩轻轻散落着一些雨滴，洒在路上和树叶上。森林左边的树荫下一片漆黑，右边的树叶在阳光下闪闪发光，湿漉漉、亮晶晶，几乎没有被微风吹动。万物复苏，夜莺鸣叫，声音时近时远，回荡在耳边。

" Yes , here in this forest was that oak with which I agreed ," thought Prince Andrew . " But where is it ?" he again wondered , gazing at the left side of the road , and without recognizing it he looked with admiration at the very oak he sought . The old oak , quite transfigured , spreading out a canopy of sappy dark - green foliage , stood rapt and slightly trembling in the rays of the evening sun . Neither gnarled fingers nor old scars nor old doubts and sorrows were any of them in evidence now . Through the hard century - old bark , even where there were no twigs , leaves had sprouted such as one could hardly believe the old veteran could have produced .

"安德鲁王子想："是的，在这片森林里，就是那棵与我约定的橡树。"可是，它在哪里呢？"他再次疑惑地望着路的左边，他没有认出那棵橡树，而是满怀敬佩地望着他要找的那棵橡树。这棵老橡树完全变了模样，树冠上铺满了深绿色的树叶，在傍晚阳光的照耀下，树冠微微颤动，伫立着。虬曲的手指、陈旧的伤疤、昔日的疑虑和忧伤，现在都不复存在。透过坚硬的百年树皮，甚至在没有树枝的地方，也长出了树叶，让人难以相信这棵老树能长出这样的叶子。

" Yes , it is the same oak ," thought Prince Andrew , and all at once he was seized by an unreasoning springtime feeling of joy and renewal . All the best moments of his life suddenly rose to his memory . Austerlitz with the lofty heavens , his wife's dead reproachful face , Pierre at the ferry , that girl thrilled by the beauty of the night , and that night itself and the moon , and .... all this rushed suddenly to his mind .

安德鲁王子心想："没错，就是这棵橡树。"顿时，他被一种春意盎然的喜悦和万物复苏的感觉攫住了。他一生中最美好的时光突然浮现在他的记忆中。奥斯特利茨与巍峨的天空，他妻子死不瞑目的责备的脸，渡口的皮埃尔，那个被夜晚的美丽所激动的女孩，还有那个夜晚本身和月亮，....，这一切都突然涌上他的心头。

" No , life is not over at thirty - one !" Prince Andrew suddenly decided finally and decisively . " It is not enough for me to know what I have in me — everyone must know it : Pierre , and that young girl who wanted to fly away into the sky , everyone must know me , so that my life may not be lived for myself alone while others live so apart from it , but so that it may be reflected in them all , and they and I may live in harmony !"

"不，三十一岁的人生还没有结束！"安德鲁王子突然最后果断地决定。"我只知道自己拥有什么是不够的，每个人都必须知道：皮埃尔，还有那个想飞上天的年轻女孩，每个人都必须了解我，这样我的生命才不会只为自己而活，而别人的生命却与之格格不入，这样我的生命才能在他们身上得到体现，他们和我才能和谐共处！"

On reaching home Prince Andrew decided to go to Petersburg that autumn and found all sorts of reasons for this decision . A whole series of sensible and logical considerations showing it to be essential for him to go to Petersburg , and even to re - enter the service , kept springing up in his mind . He could not now understand how he could ever even have doubted the necessity of taking an active share in life , just as a month before he had not understood how the idea of leaving the quiet country could ever enter his head . It now seemed clear to him that all his experience of life must be senselessly wasted unless he applied it to some kind of work and again played an active part in life . He did not even remember how formerly , on the strength of similar wretched logical arguments , it had seemed obvious that he would be degrading himself if he now , after the lessons he had had in life , allowed himself to believe in the possibility of being useful and in the possibility of happiness or love . Now reason suggested quite the opposite . After that journey to Ryazan he found the country dull ; his former pursuits no longer interested him , and often when sitting alone in his study he got up , went to the mirror , and gazed a long time at his own face . Then he would turn away to the portrait of his dead Lise, who with hair curled a la grecque looked tenderly and gaily at him out of the gilt frame . She did not now say those former terrible words to him , but looked simply , merrily , and inquisitively at him . And Prince Andrew , crossing his arms behind him , long paced the room , now frowning , now smiling , as he reflected on those irrational , inexpressible thoughts , secret as a crime , which altered his whole life and were connected with Pierre , with fame , with the girl at the window , the oak , and woman's beauty and love . And if anyone came into his room at such moments he was particularly cold , stern , and above all unpleasantly logical .

安德鲁王子回家后，决定在那个秋天去彼得堡，并为这个决定找到了各种理由。他的脑海中不断涌现出一系列合情合理的考虑，表明他必须去彼得堡，甚至必须重新服役。他现在不明白，自己怎么会怀疑有必要积极参与生活，就像一个月前他不明白自己怎么会有离开宁静乡村的想法一样。现在，他似乎明白了，除非他把所有的生活经验都用在某种工作上，重新积极地参与到生活中来，否则他所有的生活经验都将毫无意义地浪费掉。他甚至不记得以前是如何根据类似的可悲的逻辑论证，认为在经历了人生的教训之后，如果现在让自己相信有可能成为有用的人，相信有可能得到幸福或爱情，那显然是在贬低自己。现在，理智告诉他恰恰相反。去梁赞旅行后，他发现这个国家很沉闷；他以前的追求不再引起他的兴趣，当他独自坐在书房里时，他常常站起来，走到镜子前，久久地凝视着自己的脸。然后，他会转过身去看他死去的丽丝的肖像，丽丝的头发卷曲着，从镀金的镜框中温柔而欢快地看着他。现在，她不再对他说以前那些可怕的话，而是单纯、快乐、好奇地看着他。安德鲁王子双手交叉放在身后，在房间里踱来踱去，时而皱眉，时而微笑，回想着那些非理性的、无法表达的想法，这些秘密就像犯罪一样，改变了他的一生，与皮埃尔、与名声、与窗前的女孩、与橡树、与女人的美丽和爱情有关。在这种时候，如果有人走进他的房间，他就会变得特别冷酷、严厉，尤其是令人不快的逻辑思维。

" My dear ," Princess Mary entering at such a moment would say , " little Nicholas can't go out today , it's very cold ."

"亲爱的，"这时进来的玛丽公主会说 "小尼古拉斯今天不能出去，天气很冷"

" If it were hot ," Prince Andrew would reply at such times very dryly to his sister , " he could go out in his smock , but as it is cold he must wear warm clothes , which were designed for that purpose . That is what follows from the fact that it is cold ; and not that a child who needs fresh air should remain at home ," he would add with extreme logic , as if punishing someone for those secret illogical emotions that stirred within him .

"如果天气热的话，"安德鲁王子会在这种时候非常干脆地回答他的姐姐，"他可以穿着罩衫出门，但因为天气冷，他必须穿上保暖的衣服，这些衣服就是为此目的而设计的。这就是天气冷的原因，而不是说一个需要新鲜空气的孩子就应该呆在家里。"他还会以极强的逻辑性补充道，仿佛是在惩罚某人内心深处蠢蠢欲动的那些不符合逻辑的情绪。

At such moments Princess Mary would think how intellectual work dries men up .

在这种时候，玛丽公主就会想，脑力劳动会让人变得多么枯燥。
